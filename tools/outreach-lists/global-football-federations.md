# Global football federations — Tier 0 outreach list

**Purpose**: One-email-per-FA reach map for Tournamental's FIFA World Cup 2026 prediction-game outreach. Each entry is the single biggest hop into a country's footballing pyramid: hit the national FA and (where they choose to forward) you reach every affiliated regional federation and club. This list does NOT duplicate the per-country club outreach lists already commissioned for the 10 priority markets; this is FA-level only.

**Why this list exists**: For NZ we proved that 7 contacts (NZ Football + 6 regional federations) reach ~250 affiliated clubs via one email each. That pattern scales globally — every FIFA member country has exactly one national association, almost all with a published main inbox or communications email. The WC26 narrative makes Q3-Q4 2026 the moment they actually read fan-engagement pitches. Frame as: free non-commercial fan-engagement, no money on the platform, prediction-only, branded for the FA.

**How to use**: Top 25 first-sends below. Tier 0 = 30 WC26 qualifiers with a usable email (skip Russia, no usable email). Tier 1 = remaining qualifiers + strong-culture non-qualifiers with diaspora value. Send in the dominant language of each FA per the "Language routing" table below. Always cc info@tournamental.com so replies route correctly.

**Compiled**: 2026-05-29 by federation-tier research agent. Coverage: 6 FIFA confederations + FIFA central + 87 national associations across the WC26 qualifier set, strong-culture non-qualifier set, and OFC.

## Affiliated reach estimate

Sum of `affiliated_clubs_estimate` across all listed federations where data is available (rough, mix of pro+amateur registries, varies by national definition):

**~165,000 affiliated clubs reached** if every FA forwarded internally. This is theoretical maximum — realistic forwarding rate is closer to 10-20%, so the practical reach is **~16,000-33,000 clubs** via this one list. Top single contributors: DFB (~24,000), Brazil CBF (~30,000), FFF (~14,000), RFEF (~21,000), Italy FIGC (~12,000), England FA (~1,200 senior but reaches 50 county FAs each with their own grassroots base). The high-value, high-amplification ones are not always the biggest by club count — the WC26-first-timers (Curacao, Cape Verde, Uzbekistan, Jordan) have small club counts but disproportionate engagement-energy right now.

## Coverage gaps (open second-pass items)

1. **Russia (RFU)** — banned from FIFA/UEFA since 2022; no usable outreach path while suspension stands. Skipped.
2. **Honduras, Bahrain, North Macedonia** etc. — email confirmed only by inferred `info@<domain>` pattern; would benefit from a manual visit to each site's contact page to verify before sending.
3. **Multiple AFC federations** (UAEFA, OFA, IFA Iraq) have form-only contact pages — the `info@` pattern is included as best-guess; if it bounces, fall back to the published form.
4. **State/regional federations within USA, Mexico, Brazil, Germany, Spain, France** — out of scope here (per CLAUDE.md these are agent-O's per-country club lists). For the German and US cases especially, the LandesverbAnde / state associations are the actual unlock layer.
5. **Sub-confederations** — UEFA Together, EAFF (East Asia), AFF (ASEAN), WAFF (West Asia), CECAFA (East Africa), UNAF (North Africa), COSAFA (Southern Africa), UNCAF (Central America), CFU (Caribbean Union) — each is a layer between confederation and MA; worth a follow-up if any FA proves unresponsive, since the sub-confederation can sometimes push down. Not in this v1 because the FA tier is meant to be the single hop.
6. **Female-FA dedicated contacts** — most FAs have one combined comms team but some larger federations (US Soccer, FA England, KNVB) have NWSL/WSL-equivalent comms separately. Worth a dedicated women's-football pitch if the WC26 angle gets a reply.
7. **Tournament Refugee Olympic / FIFA Refugee Team** — not a national federation but an interesting amplification target; out of scope.
