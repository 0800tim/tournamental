# Spanish-language football clubs - outreach list (ES / AR / MX)

**Purpose:** invite Spanish-language football clubs, regional federations, university teams, and supporter groups across Spain, Argentina, and Mexico to spin up a free, branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<club-slug>`. Tournamental is a free-to-play tournament prediction platform run by Tournamental (a subsidiary of Growth Spurt Ltd, Auckland, NZ). Native Spanish (Castilian + Rioplatense) is supported, and the tool is non-commercial - no money changes hands, just bragging rights and a deeper match-day connection between a club and its fanbase during the 2026 World Cup.

**Language angle:** Spain + Argentina + Mexico are three of the largest football cultures on Earth, and they share the same primary fan language. A single Castilian-Spanish outreach template covers ~90% of the audience cleanly. For Argentina, use the Rioplatense variant ("vos sos", "el Mundial", "queremos invitarte" instead of "queremos invitarles") to feel less like a translated EU brochure. The word "Mundial" works everywhere; "Copa del Mundo" reads as European-formal.

**Non-commercial framing:** every contact below should hear within the first sentence that the platform is free, that no payment or sponsor obligation is asked, that the club gets a branded landing page they can share on their own channels, and that all source code is open-source under Apache 2.0. Federations and large peñas care a lot about this; treat it as the unlock condition rather than a footnote.

**Scope choice:** we deliberately skip top-flight clubs in all three countries (LaLiga, Liga MX top tier, Primera División Argentina) because they have agencies and PR pipelines that filter cold outreach. Instead we focus on tiers 2-4 (semi-pro / regional), regional federations / state associations, university teams, and diaspora supporter clubs ("peñas" / "penyes"). The federations / state associations are the **highest-leverage tier** - one email there can flow to hundreds of clubs underneath.

**Compiled:** 2026-05-29 by research agent. Coverage: 280+ entries. Real public data only. Where a contact email could not be confirmed, the row shows "(via website form)" or routes via the parent federation's switchboard - both are honest and avoid making up addresses.

## Spanish template hook

**Castilian Spanish (Spain + most of LATAM):**

> Hola [federación/club],
>
> Soy [nombre] de Tournamental, una plataforma gratuita para hacer porras del Mundial 2026. Es de código abierto, no se cobra nada, y cualquier club puede tener su propio pool con la página `play.tournamental.com/s/[slug-del-club]` para compartir entre socios, peñas y aficionados.
>
> Pensamos que podría encajar bien con [club/federación] de cara al Mundial. ¿Os interesa que os pasemos un demo de cinco minutos?
>
> Un saludo.

**Rioplatense variant (Argentina, Uruguay):**
- Swap "vosotros" / "os" → "ustedes" / "les". So `¿Os interesa...?` becomes `¿Les interesa...?`.
- Tutear with **vos**: `¿podemos pasarte un demo?` rather than `¿podemos pasarles un demo?` when writing to one named person.
- Drop "encajar bien" → "venir bárbaro" or "venir piola" for a more local feel (but keep formal in a cold first email to a federation).
- "Mundial" is universal. Avoid "Copa del Mundo" - too formal/EU.

**Mexican Spanish:**
- Castilian template works as-is.
- "Porra" works (means supporter group / pool), but in MX "quiniela" is the more common word for a prediction pool. Use **quiniela** in the MX version.
- "Aficionados" preferred over "socios" (MX clubs are less socio-driven than Spain).

## Coverage gaps & future work

This list intentionally covers **only ES + AR + MX** per the brief. Spanish-speaking football cultures outside that trio are out of scope for this pass but are high-value follow-ups:

- **Colombia** - DIMAYOR, Categoría Primera B, Categoría Primera C, departmental federations (Antioquia, Valle del Cauca, Atlántico, Bogotá D.C., etc.).
- **Chile** - ANFP, Primera B, Segunda División Profesional, Tercera A/B, regional asociaciones.
- **Peru** - FPF, Liga 2, Copa Perú regional leagues (Ligas Departamentales).
- **Uruguay** - AUF, Segunda División Profesional, Segunda División Amateur, OFI (interior).
- **Ecuador, Paraguay, Bolivia, Venezuela, Costa Rica, Panamá, Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras, Nicaragua, Dominicana, Cuba, Puerto Rico** - every one has a national federation + regional structure that could be added next pass.
- **US Hispanic football leagues** - Liga Mexicana de Sur California, Liga Hispana de Texas, Liga Salvadoreña Nueva York, etc.

Within the three countries covered:

- **Per-club secretariat emails** for the long tail of Tercera Federación (ES), Federal A / Primera C / D (AR), and Liga TDP (MX) require drilling into each individual club's website or Facebook page. The federations / state associations / ligas regionales are the right proxy for that long tail.
- **Diaspora peñas** beyond UK/US/Sydney are under-covered; Berlin, Buenos Aires, Mexico City, Miami, Houston, Madrid (LATAM peñas in Spain) all have Spanish-language football communities worth a second pass.
- **Female football** clubs (Liga F in ES, Primera División Femenina in AR, Liga MX Femenil feeder) are entirely separate pyramids and would need their own outreach pass.
