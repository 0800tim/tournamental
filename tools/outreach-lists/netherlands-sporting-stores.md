# Netherlands sporting stores outreach list

**Purpose**: Outreach to Dutch sporting goods retailers inviting them to sponsor a free, branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<store-slug>`. The prize pack is their own stock (an Adidas WC2026 match ball, an Oranje replica, a couple of pairs of boots). Non-commercial: Tournamental gives the engagement tool free; the store provides prizes from existing inventory and drives foot traffic. Frame as a foot-traffic + loyalty re-engagement play during the 14-day pre-WC launch blitz. Outreach can run in Dutch or English; nearly all NL retail head offices operate fluently in English.

**Coverage**: 33 stores spread across the national big-box chains, the football specialist e-tailers, B2B teamwear suppliers with reach into amateur clubs, and the club-retail arms of the Eredivisie and KKD. The list deliberately skips outdoor specialists (Bever, Kathmandu equivalents) and skate or sneaker boutiques that do not stock licensed WC2026 inventory. Perry Sport and Aktiesport (bankrupt 2023) are not listed under those names; their real-estate footprint now sits under the Sports World / Sports Direct fascia run by Frasers Group, which is covered. Owner-operator names are surfaced where public (Smit-Cruyff in Amsterdam is the Cruyff family PR hook). All contacts listed are verified through the retailer's own published website or a credible third-party (LinkedIn, Wikipedia, KvK, ACM); inferred-pattern emails are explicitly flagged "(unverified)" in the notes.

**Compiled**: 2026-05-31 by research agent.

## Tier 1 , National big-box chains (large reach, slower internal sign-off)

The Dutch big-box sporting tier was reshaped in 2023-2024: Perry Sport, Aktiesport and Sprinter all went under in December 2023, and Frasers Group (UK) bought the brand IP, took over ~5 ex-Sprinter stores, acquired Twin Sport Holding BV in May 2024, and rolled out Sports World as the consolidated NL fascia (9 new stores in 2024 incl. flagship at Johan Cruijff Boulevard 51 Amsterdam). The surviving non-Frasers chains are Decathlon Nederland (the volume leader on Oranje replica), Intersport Nederland (franchise co-op, ~60 members), Foot Locker EMEA (now HQ'd in Utrecht), JD Sports, Snipes, Sport 2000 Nederland (Bene Sita Trading), DAKA, and Score. One outreach to each head office covers the bulk of NL physical retail.

- **Decathlon Nederland** , chain, ~25 stores nationwide. `klantenservice@decathlon.com`. Largest sports big-box in NL with own-brand Kipsta football line plus Nike/adidas; will be the volume retailer for Oranje WC2026 replica kit, shin guards and training balls; press team actively pushes campaigns through the Persberichten page.
- **Intersport Nederland** , franchise co-op, ~60 stores. `klantenservice@intersport.nl`. Largest sports franchise co-op in NL via INTERSPORT Nederland BV; member stores carry Nike/adidas/Puma WC2026 boots and replica kits; co-op model means individual franchisees can sign up to a prediction-pool promo without head-office bottleneck. Paul Segers is Marketing en E-commerce Manager (per RocketReach).
- **Foot Locker Nederland** , chain, ~50 stores plus EMEA HQ in Utrecht. `vragen@footlocker.eu`. Mall-anchored sneaker giant; carries WC2026 Adidas + Nike replica jerseys and lifestyle football crossover; EMEA HQ moved to Creative Valley Utrecht so NL marketing decisions are local. Klantenservice mailbox services all EMEA so a NL-marketing inbox is not public.
- **JD Sports Nederland** , chain, ~30 stores. `onlinehulp@jdsports.nl`. UK sneaker chain with deep WC2026 Nike/adidas/Puma boot and replica jersey stock; mall foot traffic; growing NL footprint post-Aktiesport vacuum. Escalation `Privacy.Support@jdplc.com`.
- **Snipes Nederland** , chain, ~25 stores. `klantenservice@snipes.nl`. Streetwear sneaker chain with strong Nike/adidas football lifestyle crossover (Mercurial, Predator); Snipes Clique loyalty programme already runs sneaker drops, so WC2026 prediction overlay is a natural fit. HQ Van Liemptstraat 10 Waalwijk.
- **Sports World (Frasers Group NL)** , chain, ~18 sites under Sports World + Sports Direct fascias. Via `help.sportsworld.nl` contact form. Frasers Group's post-Perry Sport NL play; opened 9 new Sports World stores in 2024 incl. flagship at Johan Cruijff Boulevard 51 Amsterdam (the Ajax precinct); deep replica kit and boot stock from Everlast, Slazenger, Karrimor plus adidas/Nike. - **DAKA Sport & Lifestyle** , chain, ~17 stores. Via klantenservice form on daka.nl. NL-owned 17-store chain plus webshop; stocks football boots and Oranje replica kit; HQ in Capelle aan den IJssel handles all NL marketing decisions so single point of contact. Strong Rotterdam region presence so natural Feyenoord adjacency.

## Tier 2 , Football specialists and e-tailers (highest conversion segment)

Pure-play football retailers convert fastest because they are already running campaigns around the WC2026 boot drops and Oranje kit launches. This tier includes the two B2B-into-clubs operators (Voetbalshop.nl with its KNVB Teamwear Programma, and 11teamsports running NL/BE/LU teamwear from Apeldoorn) plus the e-tail specialists (Voetbalfanshop.nl, VoetbalDirect.nl, Subside Sports, Unisport.nl, Soccerfanshop.nl) and the brand-led retro specialist COPA Football in Amsterdam. A yes here unlocks supporter-base distribution that no big-box chain can match.

- **Voetbalshop.nl** , e-tailer, HQ Utrecht + national delivery. `klantenservice@voetbalshop.nl` (commercial: `teamwear@voetbalshop.nl`). Self-styled "grootste voetbalwinkel van Nederland"; runs official KNVB Teamwear Programme into amateur clubs; massive Oranje + international team replica catalogue; teamwear team already has direct lines to club committees.
- **KNVB Shop** , club-retail / federation, online only. Via onsoranje.nl contact form. Official Dutch FA webshop for Oranje and Leeuwinnen kit; Nike licensee; primary destination for fans buying the WC2026 home and away jerseys; federation has direct push channels into supporters database.
- **Voetbalfanshop.nl** , e-tailer, national online. `customerservice@voetbalfanshop.nl`. More Footballs BV (Deventer); affordable official merch focused on European clubs plus Oranje; runs the official online fanshops for several Eredivisie clubs; pure-play football retailer = fast yes.

- **Unisport.nl** , e-tailer, online (DK parent). Via faq/account-voorwaarden/contactgegevens form. Danish-founded (1995) "world's leading online football store"; full Oranje + national-team catalogue plus boots and goalkeeper gloves; runs Unisport community content so prediction-pool overlay aligns with their fan-engagement playbook.

## Tier 3 , Club-retail arms (Eredivisie + KKD official stores)

Each professional Dutch club operates an official fanshop (stadium store + online, sometimes also a city-centre site). These are the cleanest fit for a branded prediction pool because the club has a captive supporter audience, a single commercial inbox, and existing weekly engagement cadence with that audience. Coverage here spans the Eredivisie top tier (Ajax, PSV, Feyenoord, AZ, FC Twente, FC Utrecht), the Groningen/Twente/Arnhem mid-table (FC Groningen, Heracles, Vitesse, NEC), and the heritage outliers (Sparta Rotterdam, Go Ahead Eagles). One yes from any of the big three (Ajax, PSV, Feyenoord) underwrites the campaign on its own.

- **Ajax Fanshop** , Amsterdam (Johan Cruijff ArenA + Kalverstraat + online). `ajaxshop@ajax.nl`. Eredivisie giant; Adidas-kitted; flagship at Johan Cruijff Boulevard 1-3 sits next to the ArenA; massive supporter base means single biggest club-retail outreach in NL; Oranje stars (Memphis, De Ligt history) drive WC2026 spillover.
