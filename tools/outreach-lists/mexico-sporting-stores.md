# Mexico sporting stores outreach list

**Purpose**: Spanish-language outreach to Mexican sporting goods retailers and Liga MX club retail arms inviting them to sponsor a branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<store-slug>`. Mexico is one of three host nations alongside the USA and Canada, and the opening match (Mexico vs South Africa at Estadio Azteca) is the single biggest football moment for Mexican retail in a generation. The prize pool is the store's own inventory (an adidas WC2026 match ball, an El Tri replica kit, Charly Liga MX jerseys, a pair of Nike Mercurial Superfly 11 World Cup boots), Tournamental never handles prize money. Frame the pitch as a footfall plus loyalty re-engagement play across the 6-week WC2026 window, anchored on the opening match at Estadio Azteca, the Mexico vs Argentina rematch buzz, and the Estadio BBVA (Monterrey) and Estadio Akron (Guadalajara) host-city activations.

**Coverage**: 37 entries spread across the four logical tiers: the big-box chains (Innovasport, Marti, Decathlon, the global brands JD/Foot Locker/Snipes, the discount giants Coppel + Dportenis + Price Shoes, the luxury department stores Liverpool + Palacio de Hierro + Sears), the Liga MX big-7 club retail arms (Club America, Chivas, Cruz Azul, Tigres, Rayados, Pumas, Toluca) plus three more Liga MX shops (Leon, Pachuca, Necaxa) and three Liga MX broader (Atlas, Santos, Xolos) plus Mazatlan and the Liga de Expansion Cancun FC, and a focused selection of CDMX football-specialist indies (El Jugador Numero 12, Es de Epoca, FUTTROLSMX, La Futbike, Culto Futbol) plus the Cancun-based Tienda Atlante and the Andean transplant Marathon Mexico. The list deliberately skips outdoor and adventure-only retailers (Doite, Coleman MX) and golf and ski specialists because none carry licensed WC2026 inventory. Contacts marked "via contact form" are routed through the retailer's own website; one inferred-pattern email is flagged "(unverified)" in the notes. 100% of named contacts are sourced from the retailer's own published website, the BMV emisora directory, or a credible third party (Wikipedia, Modaes, Expansion, El Financiero, El CEO).

**Compiled**: 2026-05-31 by research agent.

## Coverage gaps

Honest list of what is thinner than ideal so a follow-up pass knows where to dig:

1. **FMF retail arm**. The Federacion Mexicana de Futbol does not appear to operate its own e-commerce shop; the El Tri replica kit channel routes through adidas.mx, the FIFA Official Store (store.fifa.com), and the third-party Mexico Fanshop (US).
