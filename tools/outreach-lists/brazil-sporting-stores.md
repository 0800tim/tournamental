# Brazil sporting stores outreach list

**Purpose**: Outreach to Brazilian sporting goods retailers and football club retail arms inviting them to host a branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<store-slug>`. Prize pool comes from the store's own inventory (Adidas Trionda official match ball, Seleção Brasileira home shirt, a couple of pairs of boots, retro Brazil 1970 jerseys for the club arms). Non-commercial: Tournamental gives the engagement tool free; the store provides prizes from existing stock and uses the pool to drive in-store footfall and online checkout volume during the eight-week pre-Copa blitz. Tournamental never handles prize money.

**Coverage**: 39 entries spread across three tiers: nine national big-box chains and e-tailers (Tier 1), ten football specialists and retro-jersey e-tailers (Tier 2), and twenty club-retail arms covering the big-twelve of Brazilian football plus Nordeste anchors (Tier 3). Portuguese-language outreach is the default; contact emails are left in Portuguese where the published inbox is Portuguese. The list is deliberate about which segment owns which Copa narrative: the chains move the Adidas Trionda and the Seleção shirt; the specialists move retro and limited-run prizes; the club arms own the matchday physical pool experience around their stadium. 100 percent of the contacts listed are verified through the retailer's own published website, parent-group IR page, official club site, or a credible third-party (Wikipedia, B3 listings, public CNPJ records). No harvested or invented addresses. Inferred-pattern emails (none in this list) would be marked "(unverified)".

**Compiled**: 2026-05-31 by research agent.

## Tier 2 -- Football and retro specialists (highest conversion segment)

Tier 2 retailers exist only to sell football, and their conversion will be the highest of any segment. They are smaller teams with faster decision cycles. The split inside Tier 2 matters: FutFanatics, Sport Atacado Brasil, and Cariocas FC sell current-season official replicas to active torcedores; RetrôMania, Liga Retrô, Retrôgol, and Brechó do Futebol sell licensed and vintage jerseys to colecionadores. The two sub-segments justify different prize pool framings (current Trionda + Seleção shirt vs. Brazil 1970 Pelé retro jersey). Mantos do Futebol is the media player in the segment: not a store but the largest editorial site about football jerseys in Brazil with 169K Instagram followers, useful as PR multiplier.

## Coverage gaps

Honest list of what is thinner than ideal so a follow-up pass knows where to dig:

1. **Named CEO/VP-level contacts for the chains**. Centauro/SBF and Decathlon have named executives publicly (Pedro Zemel, Gustavo Furtado at SBF) but Authentic Feet, Artwalk, World Tennis, and Paquetá Esportes do not surface a named CEO/marketing director publicly. A LinkedIn pass on "Diretor Marketing <chain>" would surface them.
2. **Per-store franchise contacts for Track&Field, World Tennis, GrêmioMania**. These all use a multi-franchisee model with hundreds of stores; each franquia can independently opt in to a pool but the head-office route was prioritised here. A regional sweep (Sul, Sudeste, Nordeste) would unlock 3x the entries.
3. **Smaller Nordeste club-retail**. Beyond Bahia, Sport, Fortaleza, and the multi-clube Recife operator, the loja oficial pages for Ceará SC, Vitória, Santa Cruz, Náutico, Sampaio Corrêa, and ABC were not separately surfaced. Each is a regional pool opportunity.
4. **Coritiba, Goiás, Cuiabá, Juventude, Bragantino, Mirassol club shops**. Skipped from the big-twelve list because their torcida footprint is smaller; worth a Tier-3.5 pass for Brasileirão Série A 2026 coverage.
5. **Marketplace seller relationships**. Magalu Marketplace, Mercado Livre Esportes, and Amazon Brasil Esporte all have category sellers who run their own catalogues -- each is potentially its own pool host but the platform-as-host conversation needs a different commercial brief.
6. **Nike Store Brasil per-location managers**. All 30+ Nike Stores in Brasil are operated by Fisia (Grupo SBF); per-store gerente emails would unlock matchday-specific Nike megastore activations but are not public.
7. **Adidas Brasil per-store managers**. Same issue: SAC central is the only public channel. Loja Roque Petroni 1089 (Morumbi Town Shopping) flagship would be the priority store-manager hit.
8. **Camisas retrô independents at scale**. RetrôMania, Liga Retrô, Retrôgol covered; FUT Retro, Bau Camisas, Fanáticos Retrô, Clube Retro, and Athleta Retrô also exist and would extend the colecionador segment.
9. **Boutique sneaker shops** (Vault, Cinch, NCSneakers). These carry crossover Nike/Adidas football lifestyle but are sneaker-culture-led; not pursued.
10. **State-specific football specialists**. The Sul (Mundo do Futebol Curitiba) and Sudeste (Inter Football proxies) are covered; specialists in BA, CE, PE, GO, DF, MG (outside Loja do Galo/Cruzeiro), AM are not.
