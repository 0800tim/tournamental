# Football clubs - DE / IT / FR / NL - outreach list (multilingual, free)

**Purpose**: invite football clubs, regional federations, university programmes and supporter groups across Germany, Italy, France and the Netherlands to spin up a free, branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<club-slug>`. The platform now ships in German, Italian, French and Dutch; this is non-commercial outreach, no sale, no money, just a tool that runs for free.

These four countries punch well above their weight in grassroots football: Germany alone has 7.4 million registered players and 24,500 clubs spread across 21 Landesverbände; Italy runs around 14,000 amateur clubs under the LND; France's FFF coordinates 14,000 clubs through 18 Ligues Régionales (13 metropolitan, 5 ultramarine); and the Netherlands routes everything through 6 KNVB districten covering ~3,000 clubs. The highest-leverage targets are not the famous top-flight clubs but the **regional federations** that already broadcast to every club in their territory - one good email to a Comitato Regionale or a Ligue Régionale puts the tool in front of hundreds of clubs at once.

The list below is organised so a human (or another agent) can run the outreach in roughly this order: regional federations first (the multipliers), then second-tier and third-tier clubs (often the most fan-engaged but least pampered by sponsors), then student football (multilingual, viral on campus), then official supporter associations (the people who already meet up to talk about football). Top-tier clubs are deliberately excluded - they have their own apps and are unlikely to embed a third-party tool. Total entries: ~548.

## Coverage gaps (second pass needed)

1. **Per-club generic emails are best-guess `info@<domain>`**. For ~80% of entries the generic `info@` is the standard pattern visible on club websites, but a manual pass through each club's `/contact` page (or its KvK / Vereinsregister / albo entry) would yield specific named secretaries (Geschäftsführer / segretario / secrétaire général / secretaris) and direct lines. Priority for that pass: the 20 first-contact targets and the regional federation list above.
2. **Bundesliga reserve teams** are listed under `info@<club>.de` of the parent club - the right contact is the `Nachwuchs-Leistungszentrum` (DE) / `settore giovanile` (IT) / `centre de formation` (FR) / `jeugdopleiding` (NL) coordinator, which usually has a dedicated email but requires drilling into each club's youth-academy site.
3. **Italian Eccellenza is per-region** (20 separate Eccellenza leagues - one per Comitato Regionale). The CSV samples 20 clubs covering 18 regions; a full pass would yield ~600+ Eccellenza clubs total. Best leverage is via the Comitati Regionali, not direct.
4. **German Oberliga is 14 parallel leagues** - only Hamburg + Bremen + Berlin + NOFV-Nord sampled. Other Oberligen (NOFV-Süd, Hessen, Niedersachsen, Westfalen, Niederrhein, Mittelrhein, Westfalen, Baden-Württemberg, Bayern Nord/Süd) total ~250+ clubs. Use the Landesverbände as proxies.
5. **French Régional 1 (R1)** - the level below National 3 - has ~250 clubs across 18 ligues. Not sampled. Same proxy story: hit the Ligue, get the cascade.
6. **Dutch Derde Klasse / Vierde Klasse** = saturday-amateur level, ~2,500 clubs. Out of scope unless KNVB districten endorse and broadcast.
7. **University programmes are central + flagship-only**. adh / CUSI / FFSU / KNVB-Studentensport coordinate but most active student clubs are independent associations whose contact details live on faculty intranets.
8. **Supporter associations** include only official, FIGC/KNVB/FFF-recognised supporter trusts. Ultras and unofficial groups are deliberately excluded - they're either anti-commercial or operationally hard to reach via email.
9. **Phone numbers and named contacts** not captured. Standard format would add `Geschäftsführer`/`segretario`/`secrétaire général`/`secretaris` name and direct mobile, but that's a manual second pass per row.
10. **Telegram / WhatsApp group invite links** for the supporter associations and regional federations would be the highest-conversion channel after an initial email - out of scope here, suggested as a follow-up enrichment pass.
