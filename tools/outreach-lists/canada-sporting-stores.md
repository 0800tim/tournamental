# Canada sporting stores outreach list

**Purpose**: Outreach to Canadian sporting goods retailers inviting them to host a free, branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<store-slug>`. Canada is one of three host nations alongside the USA and Mexico, with BMO Field (Toronto), BC Place (Vancouver) and Olympic Stadium (Montreal) all on the WC2026 venue list. The prize pack is the store's own inventory: an adidas Trionda WC2026 match ball, a Canada Soccer Mens (Les Rouges) replica jersey, a pair of Alphonso Davies-signature boots, and a CF Montréal or Whitecaps club kit where relevant. Non-commercial: Tournamental supplies the engagement tool free; the store supplies prizes from existing stock and drives foot traffic. Frame as a foot-traffic plus loyalty-re-engagement play during the 14-day pre-WC launch blitz, with bilingual (EN / FR) creative for Quebec partners.

**Coverage**: 32 retailers grouped across the big-box national chains, dedicated football specialists, online-first e-tailers, and the club-retail arms of every MLS Canadian side plus most CPL clubs. The list deliberately skips outdoor-only retailers (Mountain Warehouse, Altitude Sports outerwear-only, Bushtukah), hockey-only specialists (Pro Hockey Life, Hockey Experts) and golf or ski specialists because none carry meaningful licensed WC2026 inventory. Quebec coverage is intentionally strong (Sports Experts, La Cabane du Soccer, Boutique Soccer Mondo, SAIL, CF Montréal Boutique, Decathlon Brossard) given Olympic Stadium hosts 7 WC2026 matches incl. Canada and Mexico group fixtures. Owner-operator names and parent-company tickers are surfaced where public.

Email accuracy: Sport Chek, Atmosphere, Sports Experts, SAIL, MEC, Decathlon and Soccer World have published or commonly-routed addresses. Several independents and Foot Locker / Champs / JD use site contact forms or inferred-pattern emails marked **(unverified)** in the CSV notes column. Always verify before send.

**Compiled**: 2026-05-31 by research agent.

## Coverage gaps

Honest list of what is thinner than ideal so a follow-up pass knows where to dig:

1. **Foot Locker / Champs / JD per-store contacts**. ~280 banner stores across the Foot Locker Inc and JD Sports Canadian families all funnel to head-office customer-service inboxes; no per-store franchise managers surfaced. A LinkedIn search on "Foot Locker Canada Marketing" and "JD Sports Canada Marketing" would surface 3 to 5 named individuals.
2. **National Sports relaunch timing**. FGL Sports closed all 17 physical stores in Sept 2023 but the brand IP remains. If they relaunch ahead of WC2026 (likely Q1 2026) the banner is worth a separate outreach.
3. **Sport Chek per-province marketing leads**. National address only; Quebec, Ontario and BC each likely have a regional marketing manager surfaceable via LinkedIn.
4. **Independent Quebec football specialists outside Montreal**. La Cabane and Soccer Mondo cover Montreal but Quebec City (Soccer Plus Lebourgneuf) and Sherbrooke / Gatineau independents are not yet listed.
5. **Independent Prairie football specialists**. Calgary (Cavalry FC merch aside) and Edmonton (FC Edmonton folded 2022) have no listed pure football specialist; Saskatchewan and Manitoba even thinner.
6. **Atlantic Canada football specialists**. HFX Wanderers covers Halifax club-retail but no Atlantic pure football specialist is listed; the market may simply not support one.
7. **Indigenous sporting retail and Northern stores**. Yukon / NWT / Nunavut and First Nations sporting retail not represented; outside the WC2026 host catchments but worth noting.
8. **Boutique sneaker shops (Livestock, Haven, Nomad, Sneaker Politics Canada, etc.)**. These carry crossover lifestyle Nike and adidas football lines but are sneaker-culture-led rather than football-led; not pursued.
9. **Walmart Canada / Costco Canada / SportsDirect.com Canadian shipping**. Mass-market generalists carry replica kit and balls but their head-office marketing teams are very unlikely to opt into a third-party prediction pool; deprioritised.
10. **Direct-to-consumer arms of adidas Canada and Nike Canada**. Brand-direct stores (adidas Yonge-Dundas, Nike Toronto Eaton Centre) carry WC2026 inventory but global brand marketing approvals would route through Portland / Herzogenaurach not Canada; not pursued at this stage.
11. **Per-store named owner-operator emails** for most independents. The contact form on each site or the info@ inbox is the published primary route; direct owner email would require a LinkedIn pass for each.
12. **CPL central commercial contact**. Outreach to the league directly (canpl.ca) might unlock all 8 CPL clubs in one conversation; not yet attempted.
