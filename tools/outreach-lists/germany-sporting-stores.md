# Germany sporting stores outreach list

**Purpose**: Outreach to German sporting goods retailers and club merchandise arms inviting them to host a free, branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<store-slug>`. The prize pack is the store's own stock (an Adidas WC2026 match ball, a Germany 26 Heimtrikot or Auswärtstrikot, a couple of pairs of Predator / Mercurial boots, club-specific merch where the partner is a Bundesliga shop). Tournamental never handles prize money; the retailer keeps every cent of any optional buy-in and pockets the foot traffic. German-language outreach where the retailer is German-speaking; English fine for Frasers Group / JD Sports / Foot Locker / Fanatics regional desks.

**Coverage**: 41 stores spread across the big-box chains (Decathlon, Intersport, SportScheck, Sport 2000, JD Sports DE, Foot Locker DE, Snipes, Galeria Sport), the football specialists (11teamsports, Kickz, Outfitter, Subside Sports, Unisport, Geomix, Fanatics DE, DFB-Fanshop), regional flagship sporthouses (Engelhorn, Schuster, Bittl, Conrad, Krumholz, Voswinkel), and the official Bundesliga club retail arms (FC Bayern, BVB, RB Leipzig, Schalke 04, Eintracht Frankfurt, Bayer 04, HSV, VfB, 1. FC Köln, Werder, Hertha, Union Berlin, VfL Wolfsburg, Hoffenheim, Mainz 05, SC Freiburg, Borussia Mönchengladbach, FC St. Pauli). All 18 Bundesliga 2025/26 club shops would be reasonable - this list covers the 18 with the loudest commercial profile. Outdoor-only retailers (Globetrotter, Schöffel direct, Bergsport) are deliberately omitted because they do not stock WC2026 inventory at meaningful scale. Ownership status, Geschäftsführer names and Handelsregister entries are surfaced where public; contact emails are verified via published Impressum unless explicitly marked (unverified) for inferred-pattern addresses.

**Compiled**: 2026-05-31 by research agent.

## Coverage gaps

Honest list of what is thinner than ideal so a follow-up pass knows where to dig:

1. **Per-store named owner-operator emails** are not consistently published for most Bundesliga Fanshops. The general `fanshop@<club>.de` or `info@<club>.de` is the public route; the named Merchandising Manager would require a LinkedIn pass.
2. **Sport 2000 per-member contacts**. The Mainhausen HQ funnels every cooperative member through `info@sport2000.de`. Individual flagship stores (sport2000-flagshipstores.de) carry their own owner emails which were not enumerated.
3. **Intersport per-member contacts**. Same structure as Sport 2000 - 1500 eG members each have their own marketing budget. Voswinkel and Krumholz are the two largest covered here; the long tail (Sport Krumholz Mayen, Intersport Schmidt, Intersport Faber etc.) would need a LinkedIn / Filialfinder pass per region.
4. **Foot Locker Germany marketing lead (named)**. No public DE marketing email; only EU-wide help centre. LinkedIn search "Foot Locker Germany Marketing" likely surfaces a DACH marketing manager.
5. **JD Sports Germany marketing lead (named)**. Only `onlinehilfe@jd-sports.de` is published. LinkedIn search "JD Sports Germany Austria Marketing" surfaces a DACH marketing director (unverified currency).
6. **Kickz current Geschäftsführer**. Website Impressum lists Thor Erben but Handelsregister shows Denis Horvat (Amsterdam) + Mark Horst-Georg Petereit (Köln) more recently. Treat both as candidates and verify before send.
7. **FC Bayern Megastore inbox**. `megastore@fcbayern.com` is a pattern guess (unverified). Safer to escalate via `fcbayern.com` contact form to FCB Marketing.
8. **RB Leipzig Fanshop inbox**. `fanshop@rbleipzig.com` is a pattern guess (unverified). Their published contact is the general `rbleipzig.com/de/kontakt`.
9. **Eintracht Frankfurt fanshop window**. The Deutsche Bank Park main shop is closed 18 May - 16 Aug 2026 for stadium summer works; route any in-store activation through the MyZeil + MTZ satellite shops or through the OUTFITTER channel during that window.
10. **2. Bundesliga and 3. Liga club shops**. Only HSV, Schalke 04, Hertha BSC and St. Pauli are listed; clubs like Hannover 96, FC Magdeburg, 1. FC Kaiserslautern, Hansa Rostock and Dynamo Dresden all run sizeable Fanshops worth a Phase 2 pass.
11. **Independent women's-football specialists**. None surfaced; the WC2026 is men's-tournament-only but a women's-Bundesliga overlay pool is a Phase 2 IDEAS.md candidate.
12. **Online-only DTC kit retailers**. trikot.com, dein-trikot-shop.de, der-fussballer.de surfaced in searches but were not validated to specialist tier - worth a Phase 2 pass.
