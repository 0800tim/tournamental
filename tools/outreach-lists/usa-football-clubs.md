# USA football clubs outreach list - Tournamental WC2026 prediction pool

**Compiled**: 2026-06-01 by research agent. 202 entries across the entire US club football pyramid plus federation, youth-state, and NCAA tiers.

## Intro (for the operator)

Tournamental is a free-to-play FIFA World Cup 2026 prediction platform. The pitch to a US football club, federation or college program is **non-commercial outreach**: offer the club a free, branded prediction pool for their members, fans, players, supporters, and parent network during the WC2026 window. The pool lives at `play.tournamental.com/s/<club-slug>`. The club picks the prize (signed kit, season tickets, club merch, a clinic with the men's or women's coach), the club keeps every opt-in data point, the club drives match-day re-engagement through the WC2026 group stage and into the knockouts, and Tournamental never handles money. No subscription, no upsell, no payment processing.

The US is one of three WC2026 co-host nations (USA + Canada + Mexico). **78 of the 104 WC2026 matches play in the United States**, across 11 host cities: New York/New Jersey (8 matches inc the Final on July 19), Los Angeles (8), Dallas (9 inc a semi-final), Atlanta (8), Houston (7), Kansas City (6), Miami (7), Philadelphia (6), San Francisco Bay Area (6), Seattle (6), Boston (7 inc a quarter-final). This is the largest sporting event in US history by ticket allocation and the moment football turns from niche to mainstream in the US retail and engagement calendar.

The US club pyramid is more fragmented than the European model. The highest-leverage single targets are **not** the top MLS clubs (Inter Miami, LAFC, LA Galaxy have agencies, brand managers, and packed marketing calendars). They are:

1. **The state youth soccer associations** (Cal South, Cal North, NTYS, FYSA, etc), each governing tens of thousands of registered youth players whose parents will be glued to the WC2026 broadcast schedule. One yes from a state assoc cascades to 200+ affiliated clubs.
2. **US Soccer Foundation and US Youth Soccer Association**, which cascade nationally.
3. **United Soccer Coaches**, the ~30K-member coaches association whose newsletter reaches every D1, D2, D3, NAIA, JUCO, high school, and youth-club coach in the US.
4. **MLS NEXT Pro + USL Championship clubs in non-MLS markets** (Detroit City FC, Sacramento Republic, Indy Eleven, Memphis 901 etc), which have the loudest supporters cultures and the most receptive front offices.
5. **The host-city MLS + NWSL clubs in WC2026 venues** (Inter Miami, LAFC, NYCFC, Atlanta United, Houston Dynamo, Sporting KC, Seattle Sounders, Philadelphia Union, New England Revolution, Bay FC, Gotham FC). The crossover with the WC2026 watch-along is exact.

## Coverage gaps (be honest)

1. **MLS Next Pro reserve sides (~24)**: most are routed via MLS parent comms, so individual listings would be duplicates. Route via the MLS parent in this list.
2. **NCAA D2 + D3 + NAIA + JUCO men's and women's soccer (~1500 programs)**: not listed individually because they cascade via United Soccer Coaches (national, tier 1) and via the NCAA conference offices for D2 (RMAC, SAC, GLIAC etc). A second-pass list could surface D2 conference offices specifically (NSCAA-affiliated coaches associations cover this).
3. **High school state athletic associations (~50)**: not listed because the youth-soccer state assocs in tier 8 cover the same audience more efficiently for a 12-18-year-old WC2026 outreach. A second pass could add NFHS state athletic association marketing inboxes.
4. **MLS NEXT (youth) clubs (~150 clubs)**: not listed individually because they cascade via MLS NEXT central comms; route via the MLS press desk.
5. **ECNL (Elite Clubs National League) clubs (~120)**: not listed individually because they cascade via US Club Soccer (tier 1).
6. **Latino-market futsal and Liga MX-affiliated supporters clubs**: the Cosmopolitan League covers some but a dedicated Spanish-language US-clubs list would route via Liga MX Querida and the various peñas (Chivas USA-style Mexican-club supporter groups in LA, Chicago, Houston, Dallas). One pass could add ~30 entries here.
7. **NCAA D1 sample bias toward East Coast**: this list is weighted toward ACC + Big Ten + SEC men's and women's because the data is most reliable there. Pac-12 / Big Ten West (Oregon Cal Washington UCLA USC Stanford) and SEC West (Arkansas LSU Auburn Alabama) could benefit from a dedicated second pass.
8. **Unverified email patterns (~52 entries)**: club staff-email patterns of `press@<club>.com` and `{first}.{last}@<club>.com` were used where the published front-of-house contact is the generic `info@<club>.com`. These are flagged inline with `(unverified)` in the notes column of the CSV. Bouncebacks should be treated as a signal to route via the published `info@` mailbox or the club website contact form instead.
9. **Phone column coverage**: ~70% of entries have a published phone number from the org's website or a credible third-party. Blanks indicate no public general line was found, which is normal for clubs that publish a contact form only.
