# Clubes de futebol de Portugal - outreach list (gratuito, multilíngue)

**Compiled**: 2026-05-28 by research agent. ~159 entries.

## Intro (English, for the operator)

Tournamental is a free-to-play FIFA World Cup 2026 prediction platform with native Portuguese support. The objective of this list is **non-commercial outreach**: offer Portuguese football clubs a free, branded prediction pool for their members during the WC window - `play.tournamental.com/s/<club-slug>` - so a club can use the platform without ever opening their wallet. No subscription, no upsell, no money. Bragging rights and member engagement only.

Portugal's amateur football is a 22-association pyramid feeding the FPF, with hundreds of clubs in Liga 2, Liga 3, Campeonato de Portugal, and the distrital championships beneath. The single highest-leverage tier is **not** the elite clubs (Benfica/Porto/Sporting/Braga have agencies and will ignore us) - it is the **22 Associações Regionais (AFs)**. One greenlight from AF Porto or AF Lisboa cascades to 200+ affiliated clubs. The 22 AFs are listed first below for exactly that reason. Beneath that, the Liga 2 / Liga 3 / Campeonato de Portugal tiers are ordered by reachability, then universities, futsal, claques, and finally the Lusophone diaspora (Cape Verde, Mozambique, Angola, Guinea-Bissau, São Tomé, East Timor, Macau, plus Casas do Benfica / Sporting / FC Porto worldwide).

Sem subscrições, sem publicidade intrusiva e o código é aberto. Se fizer sentido, respondemos a este email com um link de demonstração em menos de 24 horas. Obrigado!

Drop-in variants:

- For an AF: replace "do [Clube]" with "da Associação de Futebol de [Região]" and emphasise that **every affiliated club** can spin up its own pool under the AF umbrella.
- For a Casa do Benfica / Sporting / FC Porto: emphasise the diaspora angle - "para os emigrantes portugueses [no Luxemburgo / em França / no Canadá]".
- For a CPLP federation (Cabo Verde, Moçambique, etc.): emphasise Portuguese-language availability and that it is non-commercial - "uma ferramenta gratuita em português para os adeptos da selecção".

## Coverage gaps (be honest)

1. **Email accuracy**: AF emails follow a uniform `geral@af<region>.pt` pattern that holds for the AFs with own domains and the larger ones, but the FPF-subdomain AFs sometimes only publish a contact form on their site rather than a public email. The pattern is the right first guess; bouncebacks should be treated as a signal to use the website contact form instead.
2. **Long-tail Campeonato de Portugal clubs (~20)**: ~28 of the 56 CP clubs are confirmed and listed individually; the remaining ~28 are uneven on the public web. Use the AF for the relevant region as proxy when an individual club's site is dead.
3. **Distrital championship clubs**: there are **hundreds** of them beneath the 22 AFs. Listing them individually is the wrong call - that's exactly why the 22 AFs are tier #1 in this list.
4. **Claque private contacts**: 19 legalised claques have no public email by design (APCVD discourages it). Outreach is via Facebook DM, parent-club comms, or in-person at home games.
5. **Women's football (Liga BPI + Campeonato Nacional Feminino)**: only covered via the FPF Departamento de Futebol Feminino email above - a dedicated pass would add ~30 women's clubs.
6. **Casas do Benfica/Sporting/Porto long tail**: there are ~250 Casas worldwide; the federations cascade to them all, so individual Casas are listed only where they are unusually large or strategically located (Luxembourg, Paris, Toronto, Newark, Auckland).
7. **Mid-tier futsal**: ~12 listed; another ~10 in Liga Placard + 2.ª Divisão Futsal could be added.
8. **University long tail**: 15 listed; the polytechnics (IPL, IPP, IPLeiria, IPBragança, IPVC, etc.) each have student associations with sports sections - another pass would add ~15.
