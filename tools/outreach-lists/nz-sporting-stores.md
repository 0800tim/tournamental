# NZ sporting stores outreach list

**Purpose**: Outreach to New Zealand sporting goods retailers inviting them to sponsor a free, branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<store-slug>`. The prize pack is their own stock (a Adidas WC2026 match ball, an All Whites or featured-nation jersey, a couple of pairs of boots). Non-commercial: Tournamental gives the engagement tool free; the store provides prizes from existing inventory and drives foot traffic. Frame as a foot-traffic + loyalty-re-engagement play during the 14-day pre-WC launch blitz.

**Coverage**: 35 stores spread across the national chains, the football specialists, distributors with B2B reach into clubs, and a focused selection of regional independents. The list deliberately skips outdoor-only retailers (Macpac, Kathmandu, Bivouac, Hunting & Fishing NZ) and golf/ski/hunting-only stores because none carry licensed WC2026 inventory. Smaller small-town Sportsworld franchisees are sampled (3 of ~15) to indicate the model. Owner-operator names are surfaced where public (e.g. Nathan Astle at Anderson & Hill in Christchurch is a deliberate PR hook). 100% of the contacts listed are verified through the retailer's own published website or a credible third-party (LinkedIn / Wikipedia / NZX); no harvested or invented addresses.

**Compiled**: 2026-05-28 by research agent.

## Coverage gaps

Honest list of what is thinner than ideal so a follow-up pass knows where to dig:

1. **Long-tail Sportsworld members**. There are roughly 15 independent NZ Sportsworld franchisees (Cambridge, Matamata, Levin, Waipukurau, Te Kuiti, Otorohanga, Hawera, etc.). Three sampled here. Each runs its own marketing so each is its own outreach.
2. **The Athlete's Foot per-store contacts**. ~15 NZ stores all funnel to Accent Group head office. Per-franchise GM emails not public. A LinkedIn search on "The Athlete's Foot NZ Franchise Manager" would surface 3-4 individuals.
3. **JD Sports NZ marketing lead (named)**. Only customercare@ is published. LinkedIn search "JD Sports New Zealand Marketing" likely surfaces a NZ marketing manager.
4. **Sports Direct NZ store list**. Frasers Group rollout still in progress; the first wave of physical locations (likely 5-10 stores in 2026) not yet listed publicly.
5. **Foot Locker NZ marketing contact**. No published NZ marketing email; only US-wide customer help center.
6. **Tauranga / Mount Maunganui independents**. Covered chains (Smiths, Stirling, Players, JD, Rebel, Sports Distributors) but no purely independent Tauranga sports store surfaced beyond the chain footprints.
7. **Northland / Whangarei independents**. Smiths Sports Shoes Whangarei is the main anchor; no purely-football independent listed. Whangarei FC may be the better route into that catchment.
8. **Boutique sneaker shops** (Vault, Black Box, Goat Auckland etc.). These carry crossover lifestyle Nike / adidas football lines but are sneaker-culture-led rather than football-led; not pursued.
9. **Per-store named owner-operator emails** for most independents. The contact form on each site is the published primary route; direct owner email would require a LinkedIn pass.
10. **Franchise-level Smiths Sports Shoes contacts**. 12 of the 14 stores are franchised. Each likely has its own opt-in marketing budget; head office contact only listed here.
