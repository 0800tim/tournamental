# UK sporting stores outreach list

**Purpose**: Outreach to UK sporting goods retailers inviting them to host a free, branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<store-slug>`. The prize pack is their own stock (an Adidas Trionda WC2026 match ball, a Three Lions or featured-nation jersey, a couple of pairs of boots). Non-commercial: Tournamental supplies the embed widget for free, the store provides prizes from existing inventory and drives footfall and online order volume. Framed as a footfall and loyalty re-engagement play during the 14-day pre-WC2026 launch blitz and the 32-day tournament window (11 June to 19 July 2026).

**Coverage**: 45 retailers across the four UK sporting retail strata that matter for WC2026 inventory: big-box chains (Sports Direct, JD Sports, Decathlon, Foot Locker, Schuh, Footasylum), football specialists (Pro:Direct, Classic Football Shirts, Subside, Soccer Box, UKSoccerShop, ProKit UK), official club-retail arms (Manchester United Megastore, Liverpool FC, Arsenal Direct, Chelsea Megastore, Man City CityStore, Spurs, Newcastle, Everton, West Ham, Aston Villa, Rangers, Celtic), and the Frasers / JD Sports group-owned satellites (Lillywhites, Lovell Soccer, Sweatshop, Size?, Footpatrol, GO Outdoors). Deliberately skips outdoor-only retailers, golf-only retailers and pure cricket retailers because none carry licensed WC2026 inventory. 100% of contacts listed are verified through the retailer's own published website, Companies House, the parent group's investor-relations site, or credible third-party press; no harvested or invented addresses. Owner / CEO names are surfaced where public (Michael Murray at Frasers, Doug Bierton at Classic Football Shirts, Simon Pretswell at UKSoccerShop, the Greaves family in Glasgow, the Macfarlanes at Up & Running).

**Compiled**: 2026-05-31 by research agent.

## Tier 1: Big-box sporting goods chains (large reach, slower internal sign-off)

These eight are the giants that will sell most of the WC2026 inventory in the UK. Sign-off cycles are slow (corporate procurement, brand approvals) but each yes is millions of impressions and dozens of doors of POS reach. Best-prepared outreach: a ready-made press release, a ready-made in-store POS mock with the retailer's brand, a ready-made widget embed code, and a single named retail contact.

- **Sports Direct (Frasers Group)** - ~470 UK doors. Will be the dominant high-street WC2026 destination for the Adidas Trionda match ball and every nation's replica jersey. Frasers Group HQ Shirebrook, CEO Michael Murray.
- **JD Sports** - ~500 UK doors. The dominant Nike/adidas football boot floor space outside of brand stores; carries every WC2026 replica drop. JD Sports Fashion plc, HQ Bury.
- **Decathlon UK** - ~40 stores. UK's deepest grassroots football value-tier inventory via own brand Kipsta. £14m flagship refit at Surrey Quays (2023). French-controlled, decisions take longer.
- **Foot Locker UK** - ~52 stores. EMEA-led; carries WC2026 Nike Mercurial / adidas Predator drops and lifestyle football crossovers. Major shopping-centre anchor stores.
- **Schuh** - 114 UK + Ireland stores. Family/casual audience overlap; stocks adidas Samba/Gazelle/Nike lifestyle ranges popular as casual-football fashion. HQ Livingston, owned by Genesco Inc (NYSE).
- **Footasylum** - ~66 UK stores. AURELIUS-owned (post the JD divestment), under-25 audience perfect for a social prediction-pool mechanic.
- **Decathlon Kipsta** - inside Decathlon doors. Dominant value-tier football brand for UK grassroots; cheap prize fulfilment.
- **GO Outdoors** - 75+ UK stores. JD Sports-owned; family-format superstore carries football boots, balls, training kit alongside camping. Same head-office unlock as JD.

4. **Soccer Scene Carnaby Street trading status**. Some third-party listings show closed; the e-comm presence is active. Confirm by calling 020 7439 0778 before assuming the physical store still trades.
5. **Newcastle United, Aston Villa, West Ham, Everton retail-team named lead**. Online customer service inboxes published; the commercial / retail-partnership lead at each club is not. LinkedIn search "Head of Retail" plus club name will surface the right name within minutes.
6. **Independent regional sports stores (Yorkshire, North-East, Wales, NI)**. The list deliberately leans national-chain + Premier League club. A genuine regional independent sweep would surface ~30 more entries across Hartlepool, Sunderland, Cardiff, Swansea, Belfast.
7. **Mitre Sports International (B2B)**. Brand rather than retailer, so excluded. But Mitre supplies grassroots clubs and could host a co-branded prize stack as a B2B partner of choice; left for a separate brand-partner sweep.
8. **Nike + adidas + Puma own-store partnerships**. NikeTown London (Oxford Circus), the adidas Originals stores (Carnaby, Brick Lane), the PUMA Carnaby Lab are brand-owned doors; these have separate brand-marketing budgets that act differently from retailer marketing. Worth a separate Tier 0 brand-partnership sweep.
9. **Sportsbreaks and other hospitality / experience retailers**. Hospitality + match-tickets businesses overlap the WC2026 audience but are not sporting-goods retailers; out of scope but worth a parallel hospitality sweep.
10. **Cricket / golf / rugby specialists**. Out of scope by brief, but at least 6-8 chain or independent retailers in those codes carry incidental football inventory and could host a WC2026 prize pool as a secondary campaign in the right week.
