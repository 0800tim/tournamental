# Canada Football Clubs - WC2026 Prediction-Pool Outreach List

A targeted outreach map for the Tournamental WC2026 prediction-pool campaign across Canadian football. Canada is one of three WC2026 co-hosts (with USA and Mexico); BMO Field (Toronto) hosts the Canadian opener, BC Place (Vancouver) hosts knockout matches, and Olympic Stadium (Montréal) remains a contingency venue. The list covers 124 entries across pro men's (MLS + CPL), pro women's (NSL), L1 Ontario, L1 BC, PLSQ, provincial and territorial federations, USports varsity programmes, and the largest community youth clubs / academies.

Pitch: "Free branded WC2026 prediction pool for your members, fans and supporters. You pick the prize, members re-engage with your club every match day, Tournamental never handles money."

## Tim, start with these (top 10 highest-leverage targets)

These are the single-yes unlocks that deliver the most downstream reach for the least outreach effort.

1. **Canada Soccer (CSA)** - national federation, WC2026 co-host body. One yes routes through all 13 provincial / territorial associations and grassroots membership well over half a million.
2. **Toronto FC** - hosts at BMO Field which is the Canadian WC2026 opener venue. MLSE-owned with the largest Canadian MLS social following and most established supporter culture.
3. **Vancouver Whitecaps FC** - BC Place is a confirmed WC2026 venue; established tech-savvy supporter base; co-located with UBC National Soccer Development Centre.
4. **CF Montréal** - bilingual French / English market; Olympic Stadium WC2026 contingency; Saputo ownership willing to partner.
5. **Canadian Premier League (CPL) head office** - one yes unlocks all 8 CPL clubs for a national overlay (8 cities, 8 supporter bases).
6. **Northern Super League (NSL) head office** - brand-new women's pro league launching 2025; WC2026 is their breakout cycle; one yes unlocks 6 founding clubs.
7. **Ontario Soccer** - 380k+ provincial registrants across 21 districts; the single largest provincial unlock in the country.
8. **BC Soccer** - WC2026 host-province federation; 150k+ registrants and direct line to L1BC + youth.
9. **League1 Ontario** - 24+ men's clubs and 22+ women's clubs in the country's biggest soccer province; single sponsor-style yes covers the whole pyramid.
10. **Soccer Quebec (Fédération de Soccer du Québec)** - 170k+ joueurs and Canada's only francophone unlock; bilingual delivery is the WC2026 angle.

## Pro Men's - MLS (3 clubs)

The three Canadian MLS sides anchor the country's pro men's footprint. Each plays out of (or near) a WC2026 venue. Their fan databases are large, season-ticket-holder oriented, and CRM-ready for an opt-in pool. Owner groups (MLSE, Saputo, Kerfoot) have done sponsor activations before.

- **Toronto FC** (Toronto ON) - hosts BMO Field, Canada's WC2026 opener venue; MLSE-owned with 1M+ social following.
- **CF Montréal** (Montréal QC) - bilingual market; Olympic Stadium WC2026 contingency; Ultras Montréal supporter group.
- **Vancouver Whitecaps FC** (Vancouver BC) - BC Place WC2026 venue; Southsiders + Curva Collective supporters.

CPL is the only nation-spanning pro pyramid; one yes from the league office propagates to all 8 clubs. ## Data quality notes

- 124 entries across 10 tier categories.
- 73 entries have verified, published contact emails (Canada Soccer info@, MLS / CPL / NSL club info@, federation info@, large clubs with published office emails).
- 51 entries marked "(unverified)" in notes - these use the standard info@ pattern inferred from the club's domain; outreach should fall back to the website contact form if the email bounces. Common in smaller L1O / L1BC / PLSQ clubs, USports athletics departments, and remote federations.
- Phone column populated where the club / federation publishes a main switchboard; left as "(via website)" otherwise.
- Athletics-department emails for USports programmes are uniformly inferred-pattern (athletics@<domain>) because individual sport contacts are not consistently published; in practice the right first email is via the men's / women's soccer coach, then escalate.
- Five PLSQ clubs are unverified-email - PLSQ does not centrally publish member club emails; outreach should go through the league office first, then club Facebook / Instagram DM as the practical fallback in Quebec.
- Several territorial federations (Yukon, NWT, Nunavut) maintain minimal websites - prioritise the contact-form route there.
