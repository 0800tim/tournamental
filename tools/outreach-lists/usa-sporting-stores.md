# USA sporting stores outreach list

**Purpose**: Outreach to US sporting goods retailers inviting them to sponsor a free, branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<store-slug>`. The prize pack is their own stock (an Adidas WC2026 match ball, a USMNT or Mexico or Argentina jersey, a couple of pairs of boots, supporter scarves). Non-commercial: Tournamental gives the engagement tool free; the store provides prizes from existing inventory, runs the pool to drive foot traffic and online sign-ups during the WC2026 window, and keeps all the captured opt-in marketing data. Frame as a four-week pre-WC2026 launch blitz plus the tournament itself (June 11 to July 19, 2026). The host-nation angle is load-bearing: the US is co-hosting alongside Canada and Mexico, eleven US cities are confirmed host venues (NYC/NJ, Los Angeles, Dallas, Atlanta, Houston, Kansas City, Miami, Philadelphia, San Francisco Bay Area, Seattle, Boston), and the Final is at MetLife Stadium on July 19. This is the largest sporting event in US history measured by ticket allocation, and the moment when football moves from niche to mainstream in the US retail calendar.

**Coverage**: 47 retailers covering the big-box national chains, the mall sneaker chains, the dedicated football specialists, the Latino-market soccer chains (the highest-conversion segment for the host-nation moment), and the MLS club retail arms in the WC2026 host cities. The list deliberately skips pure outdoor retailers (Bass Pro Shops, Cabela's, Recreational Equipment hunting categories), golf-only chains (PGA Tour Superstore, Edwin Watts) and ski/snowboard specialists because none carry licensed WC2026 inventory. Club-retail arms are deliberately weighted toward host-city MLS sides (Inter Miami, LA Galaxy, LAFC, Atlanta United, Seattle Sounders, NYCFC, NY Red Bulls, FC Dallas, Houston Dynamo, Sporting KC, Philadelphia Union, NE Revolution) because they will run their own watch-along and viewing-party programmes through WC2026 and a prediction-pool layer plugs in naturally. Owner-operator names are surfaced where public (Niky Familia at Niky's Sports in LA; the Mersho brothers at Shoe Palace; David Beckham + Jorge Mas at Inter Miami) because those are press hooks for the campaign launch, not just outreach. Most contact emails are verified through the retailer's own published website or a credible third-party (SEC filings, LinkedIn, press releases); a handful of MLS team-store inbox addresses follow an unverified pattern and are marked accordingly in the notes column.

**Compiled**: 2026-05-31 by research agent.

## Tier 1 - Big-box chains (large reach, slower internal sign-off)

These nine chains hold an outsized share of US sporting inventory and footfall. None are football-first, but every one of them will be heavily stocking the Adidas WC2026 match ball, the USMNT replica kit, and the eleven WC2026 competing-nation jerseys most likely to move in their footprint (Mexico in the South and West, Argentina in the Northeast and Florida, Brazil in the Northeast and South Florida, Germany / Italy / Portugal in Northeast diaspora corridors). The Sun Belt chains (Academy Sports, Hibbett, Shoe Palace, DTLR) are weighted toward markets with strong Latino demographics and toward WC2026 host states (Texas, Florida, California, Georgia). Internal sign-off at this tier runs through corporate Marketing or Brand functions and takes longer than a specialist's owner-operator yes, so timing the outreach is important. Aim for late summer 2025 contact so the campaign can be in their FY26 marketing plan.

## Coverage gaps

Honest list of what is thinner than ideal so a follow-up pass knows where to dig:

1. **Per-store named GMs at the big-box chains.** Dick's, Academy and Hibbett each have store-level GMs with community-event budgets. Corporate Marketing approval would still gate any signage but per-store opt-in pools are possible. A LinkedIn search on each chain's "Community Marketing Manager" surfaces 5-10 named individuals per chain.
2. **DSG / Going Going Gone / Public Lands sub-banners of Dick's.** Dick's House of Sport, Going Going Gone (off-price), Public Lands (outdoor) and Moosejaw all roll up to DKS but have separate marketing budgets. Not pursued.
3. **Modell's contact verification.** The relaunched Modell's is run by Retail Ecommerce Ventures; the published contact route is a website form rather than a direct inbox. Email above is an unverified pattern.
4. **Foot Locker St Petersburg HQ transition.** Foot Locker Inc is moving its HQ from NYC to St Petersburg FL through 2025-26; if the move affects the Marketing org chart it may impact response time.
5. **MLS team-store named contacts.** The team-store inboxes above are unverified-pattern guesses. Primary route in practice is the contact form on each club's official site. A LinkedIn search on each club's "Pro Shop Manager" or "Retail Operations Manager" surfaces 1-2 named individuals per club.
6. **Fanatics single-point-of-contact.** Fanatics Inc runs the e-commerce backend for US Soccer Store, KitBag, World Soccer Shop, Inter Miami CF Team Store and arguably more. A Fanatics-level conversation could unlock all of them at once; not pursued because Fanatics is sensitive about partner programmes and would need a Tournamental-side commercial deck first.
7. **Long-tail Latino-market soccer shops.** Niky's, SoccerLoco, Premier Soccer and Calcio Sport are the big ones. There are 20-30 smaller Latino-market soccer shops in Southern California (Pico Rivera, Huntington Park, Long Beach), Texas (Houston, Dallas, San Antonio), South Florida (Hialeah, Doral, Kissimmee) and Chicago (Little Village, Pilsen) that each run owner-operator with strong local foot traffic but no national online presence. Worth a separate Spanish-language outreach campaign.
8. **MLS NEXT Pro + USL Championship club retail.** Below MLS there are 24 USL Championship clubs and 30+ MLS NEXT Pro clubs, each with a small online shop and watch-along audience. Not pursued for the first wave because the per-club return is small but a templated email blast could pick up 5-10 of them.
9. **College athletics retail.** US college soccer programmes (UCLA, Indiana, Stanford, Notre Dame, North Carolina) run their own retail through Fanatics + the campus bookstore channel. Not pursued because the demo skews younger and is more easily reached through a campus channel.
10. **US Soccer Federation direct.** The US Soccer Store is included but the Federation itself has a sponsorship / commercial team that could endorse the campaign nationally. Direct USSF press contact: communications@ussoccer.org. Not pursued for the first wave because USSF approvals run long.
11. **Off-price clearance channels (TJ Maxx, Marshalls, Burlington, Ross).** These off-price chains clear large volumes of last-season football boots + replica jerseys. Not direct outreach targets because their vendor relationships sit at the corporate buying-office level and a prediction-pool tie-in is not their model. Useful context for inventory clearance windows post-WC2026.
12. **Mom-and-pop community soccer-coach stores.** Many youth-soccer clubs have a single coach-run shop or kit-printing operation rather than a retail storefront. Best approached via the USYSA channel through a Soccer.com / Eurosport partnership (#2 above) rather than directly.
