# UK football clubs + associations outreach list (non-commercial)

## What this is

This is a non-commercial outreach list for Tournamental, a free, multilingual FIFA World Cup 2026 prediction platform. We are not selling anything. We are offering grassroots clubs, county FAs, schools associations, supporters trusts and university teams a free, branded prediction pool their members can use during the WC window at `play.tournamental.com/s/<club-slug>`. No money changes hands. The goal is to get the tool into the hands of communities that could use a bit of free fan-engagement gas during the WC, and to seed organic word of mouth in real football communities.

## Why these tiers (and why not Premier League / EFL)

Pro clubs in the EFL and above won't engage with a free tool from cold outreach. Their fan engagement runs through agencies and existing CRM systems, so they are skipped entirely. The leverage instead lies with: (1) **County FAs** in England, which each fan out to between 200 and 2000 affiliated grassroots clubs and have one comms manager who can blast a newsletter; (2) **Schools FAs** which reach the youth-football audience that loves a prediction game; (3) **National League / Steps 2 to 4** non-league clubs which have a single club secretary doing all comms and a loyal local fanbase; (4) **Supporters trusts** which are member-run, peer-to-peer organisations that share their own marketing voice. The big hit ratios will come from CFAs and schools associations, with individual clubs as the warm follow-ups and proof.

## How to use this list

Work the **Highest-leverage first 20** below first, in order. After that, drop into the per-tier sections and contact in geographic clusters (one county at a time) so the local press has a chance to pick up a regional pattern. Always personalise the opening line: reference a recent result, the manager's name, or a specific club fixture. Never bulk-send. Aim for under 100 emails per day so the operator can answer replies in real time.

## Voice and template

Voice is **peer-to-peer**, like one fan emailing another. Mention "your club secretary" or "your committee", not "your CEO". Lead with one specific detail you've checked (last weekend's result, a recent FA Cup tie, the date of their next fixture, a notable player). Never say "we are pleased to offer" or "as a marketing partner". Keep messages under 120 words. Make it dead obvious that this is free, takes 60 seconds to set up, and they own everything that happens inside their pool. Sign off with a first name and a personal mobile if you'd like a reply.
