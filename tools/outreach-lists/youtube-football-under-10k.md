## Coverage gaps & caveats

- Betting-spam filter excluded 29 channels (descriptions matched 2+ of: betting tips, sure win, telegram, VIP, 100% win, odds, tipster, predictions guaranteed, 💰, jackpot, sure odds, fixed match, free tips). If Tim wants to revisit any of these, they're still in `channels.json` — drop the spam check from the filter.
- Re-upload / highlights-farm filter excluded 1 channels matching name/desc hints ('Full Match Replays', 'All Goals HD', 'Extended Highlights', etc).
- Inactivity filter excluded 323 channels with no upload in the last 60 days, and 67 with <1 upload/month.
- Subscriber filter excluded 327 channels outside the (0, 10000) band — either zero/missing subs, or already above the 10k cap.
- Low-traffic filter excluded 13 channels with ≤100 lifetime views (abandoned hobby uploads).
- Tier classification is heuristic — based on keywords in channel name and first ~500 chars of description. A human reviewer should re-tier the top 20-30 before send for accuracy.
- Email coverage is partial: YouTube does not expose About-page emails to scrapers. Only emails that creators volunteered in their description are captured here. For the rest, manual About-page visits are required.
- Language detection comes from `primary_language` in the cache; some channels in 'Other' are still effectively English but were misclassified by YouTube's API.
- No fresh discovery was run (would have cost YouTube API quota). If <100 channels here feel too thin, the cache can be re-seeded with niche queries (women's football, futsal, lower-league, fantasy analysis) via `tools/youtube-discovery/discover.py`.
