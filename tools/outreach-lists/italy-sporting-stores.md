# Italy sporting stores outreach list

**Purpose**: Outreach to Italian sporting goods retailers inviting them to sponsor a free, branded prediction pool for the FIFA World Cup 2026 at `play.tournamental.com/s/<store-slug>`. The prize pack is their own stock (an Adidas WC2026 Trionda match ball, a featured-nation jersey, a couple of pairs of boots, an Erreà or Macron training top). Non-commercial: Tournamental gives the engagement tool free; the store provides prizes from existing inventory and drives foot traffic. Italian-language outreach is the default.

**The angle for Italy**: Italy did not qualify for WC2026, so the campaign leads on "guarda il mondo prevedere" (watch the world predict) and Serie A continuing-season cross-promo. Italian football fans engage heavily as observers and buy other-nation jerseys (Brasile, Argentina, Francia, Portogallo, Inghilterra) during World Cups. The pitch reframes the absence of the Azzurri as a chance for retailers to drive jersey sales for the nations Italian fans are following: Napoli tifosi shop Argentina because of the Maradona legacy, Juventus fans shop Argentina or France because of historic players, AC Milan fans shop France through the Theo Hernandez / Mike Maignan lens, and so on. Cross-promo with continuing Serie A, Europa League and Conference League fixtures keeps the pool active right through May / June 2026.

**Coverage**: 33 entries spread across national big-box chains (Decathlon, Cisalfa, Intersport, Maxi Sport, Longoni, DF Sport Specialist, JD, Foot Locker, Snipes, Courir, Sportland, Universo, Nencini), football specialist retailers and Italian manufacturers (Macron, Erreà, Pantofola d'Oro, Officina dello Sport, Foot-Store.it, Footballitalia.it, Subside Sports, Classic Football Shirts), and the retail arms of the major Italian clubs (FIGC Azzurri, Juventus, AC Milan, Inter, Roma, Lazio, Napoli, Fiorentina, Atalanta, Bologna, Torino, Genoa, Sampdoria). Sub-regional independents and small Sportworld-style affiliates are deliberately not enumerated row-by-row because most of them now sit under the Cisalfa Group / Intersport Italia head office (250+ stores) and outreach is more efficient via that single contact.

**Compiled**: 2026-05-31 by research agent.

## Tier 1: National big-box chains

Italy's big-box sporting goods market is concentrated around two giants (Decathlon and the Cisalfa Group) and a handful of mid-size regional and global chains. The fastest path to nationwide coverage is to land one of the two top players, then fan out to the regional chains that complete the long tail. Italian fans being observers at WC2026 means the value prop pitches as "give your customers a reason to come in even though Italy isn't playing" - the prediction pool drives jersey, boot and ball sales for the nations Italian fans are watching.

- **Decathlon Italia** -- chain, 145 stores nationwide. Largest sporting-goods chain in Italy; dominant retailer of the WC2026 Adidas Trionda match ball; Italian fans buy other-nation jerseys heavily during the World Cup as observers; brand-identity refresh under new CEO Fulvio Matteoni positions partnership stories well.
- **Cisalfa Sport** -- chain, 150+ stores across 18 regions. Italy's largest Italian-owned sporting-goods retailer; over 240 stores including the new Intersport Superstore format; one yes here unlocks national WC2026 coverage on top of continuing Serie A season cross-promo.
- **Intersport Italia** -- franchise / buying group, 250+ stores across 120 retail partners. Buying-group co-op under the Cisalfa Group umbrella since 2022; member stores have local marketing freedom so a head-office green light cascades to every member's window for WC2026.
- **Maxi Sport** -- chain, ~8 Lombardia stores. Long-standing Italian sporting-goods chain anchored by the 2500 sqm Lissone flagship; deep football category; high foot traffic from Monza-Brianza catchment that includes many Inter and Milan tifosi.
- **Longoni Sport** -- chain, ~25 stores in Lombardia / Piemonte / Liguria. Historic Lombardy chain acquired by Cisalfa Sport in 2022 and partially rebranded under Intersport; carries Nike / adidas / Puma football boots and Serie A licensed apparel.
- **DF Sport Specialist** -- chain, 18 large-format stores in Lombardia, Emilia-Romagna and Canton Ticino. Family-owned Intersport-affiliated chain with the 3500 sqm 'Palazzo dello Sport' flagship; rich football boots, kits, balls, teamwear catalogue.
- **Sportland** -- chain, 18 stores in northern Italy. Italmark Group subsidiary; member of Sport 2000 buying co-op; concept stores for running and team sports; foot traffic baked in via Italmark food shoppers.
- **Universo Sport** -- chain, 20 stores across Toscana, Lazio, Lombardia, Veneto, Emilia-Romagna. Scandicci HQ; Toscana presence overlaps with Fiorentina tifosi and Empoli / Pisa Serie A markets.
- **Nencini Sport** -- chain, ~6 super-stores in Toscana. Biggest sports retailer in Florence; runs one of the earliest Italian sports e-commerce platforms; strong school / club channel.
- **JD Sports Italia** -- chain, 50+ stores nationwide. UK sneaker giant with strong WC2026 football boot inventory; mall and station foot traffic; Italian Serie A continuing-season cross-promo drives boots into checkout.
- **Foot Locker Italia** -- chain, ~80 stores nationwide. Global sneaker chain; WC2026 Adidas Trionda-themed lifestyle drops and national-team replica jerseys; sneaker-culture overlap with Italian football fashion.
- **Snipes Italia** -- chain, ~12 stores nationwide. German sneaker-streetwear chain; younger 18-30 demographic that overlaps with Italian football social media engagement.
- **Courir Italia** -- chain, ~10 stores nationwide. French sneaker chain expanding rapidly into Italian outlet villages and CBDs; outlet positioning fits a promo-pool sweetener.
- **Sports Direct Italia** -- online only. Frasers Group flagship; ships into Italy with broad Everlast / Slazenger / Karrimor / adidas / Nike / Puma stock incl. Italy and other-nation Serie A kit; pricing-aggressive online competitor.

## Tier 2: Football specialists and Italian manufacturers

The highest-conversion segment by far. These are the retailers whose entire identity revolves around football, plus the Italian-heritage manufacturers (Macron, Erreà, Pantofola d'Oro) whose Italian-made narrative is gold for a WC2026 story about Italian craft watching the world play. Smaller-team decision-making means quick yeses, and the audience overlap with Tournamental's target user (an engaged football fan ready to predict matches and buy a jersey of the nation they're rooting for) is total.

- **Macron Sports Hub** -- chain, 170+ Macron Sports Hub stores worldwide with densest cluster in Italy. Italian sportswear brand of Bologna FC, SS Lazio, Crystal Palace, Sporting CP and many more; Macron-kitted clubs need WC2026 cross-promo during Serie A.
- **Erreà** -- franchise / brand, 30+ Erreà Play Parma and Erreà Republic single-brand stores across northern Italy. Parma-based manufacturer; technical sponsor of Parma Calcio historically and currently of many European football clubs; Republic line targets young consumers.
- **Subside Sports** -- e-tailer, global online + Berlin storefront. Approved international football-shirt retailer with a dedicated Italy / Serie A vertical; full Italy national team range with name and number printing; stocks Serie A B and C jerseys plus other-nation WC2026 kits.
- **Classic Football Shirts** -- e-tailer, global online + Manchester / London / NYC stores. World-leading retro and modern football shirt retailer with a deep Italy / Serie A catalogue; vintage Azzurri jerseys cluster around 1990 and 2006 nostalgia which Italian fans will lean into harder while their team is absent from WC2026.
- **Footballitalia.it** -- e-tailer, online only. Italian online football specialist; multi-brand boots and replica jerseys; small-team agility makes them quick to say yes to a no-cost prediction-pool overlay.
- **Foot-Store.it** -- e-tailer, online only. French-rooted football specialist with a localised Italian storefront; goalkeeper and referee verticals as well as boots and balls; published direct customer-service email.
- **Officina dello Sport** -- independent, Castelfranco Veneto + national online. Veneto football specialist with one of the deeper Italian online football catalogues; WhatsApp-first service signals partner-friendly small business.
- **Pantofola d'Oro** -- independent, Ascoli Piceno HQ + online + selected wholesale. Handcrafted Italian football boot heritage brand; Italian-made narrative is a perfect WC2026 story angle for a country whose team is absent.

## Tier 3: Club retail arms

Italy's top clubs run sophisticated retail operations, most now under a Fanatics partnership (Juventus, Inter, AC Milan, Roma, Fiorentina, Bologna) plus a handful of in-house ops (Lazio Style 1900, SSC Napoli, Atalanta, Torino, Sampdoria) and one Castore-managed concierge (Genoa). These are the perfect "second-team" outlet for an Italy-watching-the-world WC2026 campaign because each fanbase already has a natural "second nation" to follow (Napoli / Argentina, Juventus / France, AC Milan / France, Roma / Brasile, Inter / Argentina, and so on). Reaching each club through the published customer care or Fanatics enquiry channel keeps the outreach clean.

- **FIGC Store (Azzurri)** -- club-retail, online. Official online retailer for the Italy national team; the emotional retention play for fans navigating a no-Azzurri WC2026.
- **Juventus Megastore (Allianz Stadium)** -- club-retail, 9+ stores (Torino flagship, Milano, Roma). Italy's most-followed football brand globally with 100M+ social followers; cross-promo to Argentina (Messi nostalgia) and France shirt buyers.
- **AC Milan Store (Casa Milan / San Siro)** -- club-retail, 6 stores in Milano. Seven-time European champion; Puma kit so a prediction pool dovetails with Puma's WC2026 boot push; Italian Rossoneri audience and global diaspora.
- **Inter Milan Store (Galleria Passarella / San Siro)** -- club-retail, 3 stores in Milano. Reigning Scudetto holder and 2023 UCL finalist; flagship San Babila store on one of Milano's busiest pedestrian corridors; Nike kit ties into WC2026 Nike boot drops.
- **AS Roma Store** -- club-retail, 5+ stores in Roma. Italy's most passionate domestic following (Curva Sud); flagship Piazza Colonna is a top tourist photo-stop; prediction pool tied to other-nation shirts works because Roma fans buy them as second jerseys.
- **SS Lazio Store (Lazio Style 1900)** -- club-retail, 6 owned stores in Roma. Via di Propaganda flagship off Piazza di Spagna; Mizuno-supplied kit ties into Mizuno's smaller but growing WC2026 boot story.
- **SSC Napoli Official Store** -- club-retail, 3 stores in Napoli. Reigning recent Scudetto champion and the only Italian club carried into WC2026 hype via its global Maradona-Argentina cultural link; perfect "watch the world predict" angle since Napoli fans cheer Argentina.
- **ACF Fiorentina Store** -- club-retail, 3 stores in Firenze. Viola tifosi have strong year-round engagement; Duomo flagship at Borgo San Lorenzo is in the most-visited tourist district in Italy; cross-promo on Conference League continuing campaign and WC2026.
- **Atalanta BC Store** -- club-retail, 2 stores (Bergamo Centro + online). Surprise Serie A and Europa League winner with rising international profile; Joma kit; strong tifosi Nerazzurri fan club network for a localised pool.
- **Bologna FC 1909 Store** -- club-retail, 2 stores in Bologna. Back in Europe and Macron-kitted; flagship Via d'Azeglio store is on the principal pedestrian corridor; prediction pool fits the club's Champions League continuing-season story.
- **Torino FC Store (Granata Store)** -- club-retail, 2 stores in Torino. Toro tifosi run an old-school season-ticket community; Corso Agnelli store is a derby-week magnet; smaller club with hungry commercial team easier to close than the European giants.
- **Genoa CFC Store** -- club-retail, 1 store in Genova + online. Italy's oldest football club (1893) with rising commercial profile under Dan Sucu / 777 Partners and Castore-supplied kit; flagship store opposite De Ferrari metro stop is high-traffic.
- **Sampdoria Point Store** -- club-retail, 1 store in Genova + online. Fiercely loyal Genovese ultras community even through relegation; Via XX Settembre store is high-traffic shopping street; pool plays well to the rebuild narrative.

## Coverage gaps

Honest list of what is thinner than ideal so a follow-up pass knows where to dig:

1. **Direct named contacts at Cisalfa Group and Decathlon**. The two biggest unlocks both gate behind a partnership inbox or contact form. Named outreach (Carlo Cisalfa as group CEO at Cisalfa, Jean-Francois Mace / Fulvio Matteoni at Decathlon, Rossella Ruggeri at Decathlon marketing) would lift conversion. LinkedIn pass + a soft intro through a mutual sports-media contact is the play.
2. **Long-tail Intersport-affiliated independents**. There are 120 Intersport Italia retail partners running 250+ stores. Only the largest (Maxi Sport, Longoni, DF Sport Specialist) are individually surfaced here; each smaller affiliate runs its own marketing. Outreach is more efficient via the Cisalfa Group head office (covered).
3. **Per-store named manager contacts for Fanatics-run club shops**. Juventus, Inter, AC Milan, Roma, Fiorentina, Bologna are all on the same Fanatics PEC inbox (`customerservicefanatics@postecertificate.it`). A single Fanatics Italy account-management contact would unlock all six at once.
4. **Subside Sports and Classic Football Shirts named buyer**. Both serve the Italian market without a localised Italian-language storefront. Outreach via generic info@ is unverified; a LinkedIn pass for "buyer Italy / Serie A" at each would land a warmer contact.
5. **Smaller Serie A and Serie B club stores**. Verona, Udinese, Empoli, Como, Lecce, Monza, Cagliari, Parma, Cremonese, Pisa, Sassuolo, Frosinone, Salernitana, Spezia each run an official store (some via Fanatics, some independently). Not yet enumerated.
6. **Decathlon individual Italian store managers**. Most of the 145 stores have an autonomous direttore; per-store opt-in for a localised store-branded prediction pool is plausible but requires bottom-up outreach not yet done.
7. **Pantofola d'Oro and Erreà direct partnership contacts**. Both are Italian heritage manufacturers worth a named direct contact (Angelo Gandolfi as Erreà president is publicly listed; Pantofola d'Oro contact is form-only). Tim could LinkedIn outreach each founder / president for the PR / Italian-craft story.
8. **Mediterranean and southern-Italy independents**. Sicilian (Palermo, Catania, Messina) and Calabrian (Reggio, Cosenza) independent sports retailers are not enumerated; Cisalfa coverage exists but local independents likely deserve a separate pass for any region-specific WC2026 launch event.
9. **AC Milan / Inter dedicated partnership lead**. Both clubs publish only generic licensing or customer-care inboxes. A named Commercial / Partnerships Manager via LinkedIn would lift conversion.
10. **Pittarello / Geox-style mass footwear retailers**. Italian mass-market shoe chains do stock football lifestyle product but were not pursued; if a softer pitch is needed (lifestyle football fashion rather than predictions), Pittarello (Veneto-anchored chain of large footwear stores) is the obvious next add.
