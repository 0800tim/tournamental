# Playbook: Federations and Governing Bodies

**Audience:** National football federations, regional federations (state, county, distrital, provincial), and confederations. The leverage segment of the entire blast: **87 verified emails** that, between them, reach an estimated **40,000+ affiliated clubs** worldwide.

**The promise to a federation:** A free, federation-branded master pool plus an opt-in invite for every affiliated club to spin up their own branded pool. The federation gets a national snapshot of the football imagination for six weeks. The clubs get a free engagement tool. Tournamental does the work.

## 1. Who they are

Three concentric circles:

1. **Confederations**: UEFA, CONMEBOL, CONCACAF, CAF, AFC, OFC, plus FIFA itself. Long-shot pitches because they are bureaucratic monoliths, but worth the comms-team email for legitimacy.
2. **National Federations**: NZ Football, US Soccer, Federazione Italiana Giuoco Calcio, AFA Argentina, FPF Portugal, KNVB, DFB, FFF, RFEF, FA, CBF, FMF Mexico, Canada Soccer. Each governs hundreds to thousands of affiliated clubs.
3. **Regional federations**: County FAs (UK), Distrital Associações (Portugal), State Youth Associations (USA), Provincial Soccer Associations (Canada), German Landesverbände, Italian regional FIGC arms, Spanish autonomous federations, Brazilian Federações Estaduais (CBF state arms), Argentinian Ligas Regionales. **This is where the real leverage lives** - a single regional federation often governs 50 to 400 amateur clubs.

The 87 entries in `global-football-federations.csv` plus the federation rows inside the country-specific clubs lists add up to the universe.

## 3. Positioning

**One sentence**: "{{COMPANY}} carries one free federation-branded master pool, every affiliated club opts in to its own branded pool, you see the leaderboard for the entire region. Free, no money handling, no admin burden on you."

**Three reasons why now**:

1. **The federation does not have to do anything operational.** We build the master pool. We send the invite to affiliated clubs from your address (or you forward it, your choice). We aggregate the leaderboard. The federation gets the credit.
2. **It is the cheapest member-service win of the year.** Affiliated clubs notice federations that give them free tools. Goodwill currency.
3. **It is a story for the federation's own comms team.** "We partnered with Tournamental to power the WC2026 prediction pool for the entire {{REGION}} football pyramid" reads like a press release.

## 4. The ask in three parts

We need three small yeses from the federation:

1. **One yes**: federation runs its own master pool at `play.tournamental.com/s/<fed-slug>` with the federation logo and colours. We build it. Free.
2. **One yes**: federation forwards a one-paragraph invite to its affiliated clubs. We draft the copy in the federation's house style; the federation reviews and forwards. (Or, if the federation prefers, we send the invite directly with the federation's name in the subject and copy them on it.)
3. **One yes** (nice-to-have, not required): federation mentions the pool in its next member-comms cycle (newsletter, AGM update, member-engagement blast).

That is the entire ask. No paperwork, no commercial agreement, no integration work.

## 6. The 24-hour federation build

Concierge build for a federation is 1 hour of our time. Standard concierge build for a club is 30 minutes. Federation build adds:

- Per-club sub-slug provisioning (each affiliated club gets `play.tournamental.com/s/<fed>-<club-slug>` if they want a sub-domain feel).
- Federation-wide leaderboard aggregation across sub-pools.
- A one-page comms kit (logo, sample social posts, sample member-email) the federation can reuse.

If the federation says yes today, the master pool can be live and ready-to-announce by tomorrow.

## 7. The invite-club-list flow

The hardest single step in the federation play is "federation forwards the invite to its affiliated clubs". We make this trivial:

**Path A (federation forwards)**: we send the federation a Word doc / Google Doc with the invite copy formatted for their newsletter. They paste it into their own mailer.

**Path B (we send, federation is bcc)**: federation gives us permission to send to their affiliated-club list (which they often publish publicly on their site anyway) with the federation's name in the subject. Federation is bcc on every send so they can see what is going out.

**Path C (federation hosts an opt-in page)**: federation posts a "register your club's pool" link on their member portal. We provide the destination page (`play.tournamental.com/federations/<fed-slug>/affiliate-signup`). Affiliated clubs self-serve onto the federation's master leaderboard.

Most federations pick Path A. We have ready-made Path B copy for the ones that pick that. Path C is operationally cleanest for big federations like USYSA.

## 10. Objections + answers

**"Our member clubs do not want another mailing-list ask from us."**

Understood. Path C (federation hosts an opt-in page on its member portal) sidesteps the email blast entirely. Clubs that want it sign up; clubs that don't, don't see anything. No new emails to the affiliated-club list.

**"We have a sponsorship deal that restricts what we can promote."**

The federation can keep all sponsorship rights intact. The pool can carry the federation's sponsor logo (concierge ask, free), or run with no sponsor at all. We do not bring our own sponsor inventory into a federation pool. White-label premium tier removes every Tournamental mark, if needed.

**"What about the affiliated clubs' data? Who owns it?"**

Each club owns its own pool data. The federation sees aggregate (number of members, leaderboard position, country breakdown). Tournamental never sells or shares any of it. Standard DPA available on request, signed in 24 hours.

**"Our federation does not endorse third-party games."**

Federation is not endorsing the platform; federation is offering a free engagement tool to its member clubs and members can opt in or out. We are happy to draft the comms in language that explicitly avoids the word "endorse". Affiliated clubs participate at their own volition.

**"What if we want the master pool to include our national team?"**

The pool covers WC2026 by design. If the federation's national team is in WC2026, the pool naturally includes them. If the federation's national team is NOT in WC2026 (Italy, Türkiye, etc.), we frame the master pool as "predict the whole tournament" and the federation can use it as a bridge engagement product. Italian federation members predict on every match, even though Italy isn't playing.

## 13. Numbers we expect

- Cold-email reply rate to federations: **15 to 25%** (highest of any segment). Federations that have published comms inboxes reply.
- That gives **15 to 25 federation yeses** from the 87-email blast.
- Of those, 60% will go Path A (forward to clubs), 30% Path B (let us send), 10% Path C (host opt-in page).
- Each federation yes converts to **20 to 200 affiliated-club pools**, depending on the federation's size.
- Total cascade: **400 to 4,000 affiliated club pools** from federation channels alone.

This is the most leveraged 87 emails of the entire blast.
