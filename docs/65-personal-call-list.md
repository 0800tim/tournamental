> **Purpose.** Tim has a finite voice. This is who to spend it on.
> A single call to one of these humans, if it lands, gets Tournamental
> in front of 100-5,000 club admins in one phone conversation. Sorted

> the people on THIS list.

## How this is scored

- **Reach**: how many clubs / members are downstream of a yes from this person.
- **Answer-rate**: realistic chance the human picks up + listens for 90 seconds.
- **Resonance**: how likely they actually care about the offer (a free, non-betting,
  open-source tool for fan engagement).
- **Effort to find their number**: direct dial published = green; switchboard only
  = amber; LinkedIn-route-only = red (probably skip in favour of email).

## Script for the personal call (90 seconds, memorise it)

> "Hi [name], it's Tim Thomas here from Auckland. I've built a free
> open-source World Cup 2026 prediction game called Tournamental, no
> money in it, no betting, no app to install. I'm calling [org] today
> because [specific reason for THIS person]. The ask is small: would
> you be willing to forward a one-page how-to to your [affiliated
> clubs / member list / readership] so any of them that want to spin
> up a free pool for their members during the WC can do it in 5
> minutes. I do all the heavy lifting on my side, you just forward.
>
> I can have a co-branded "[Org] WC26" pool in your inbox in an hour
> if you say yes today. Want me to send you the demo URL while we're
> on the call?"

If you stumble, the words "free, open-source, no betting, no app"
are the four magic phrases. Get them out inside the first 15 seconds.

## What to do on the call

1. **First 30 seconds**: name + 1 sentence about Tournamental + the
   ONE thing you want from them (forward a how-to / co-brand a pool /
   write a 200-word post).
2. **Next 30 seconds**: their objection (cost, risk, time, "we have
   our own app"). Answer in one line each, don't elaborate.
3. **Last 30 seconds**: close. Either "great, I'll send the link now,
   what's your best email?" or "OK, who else at [org] should I talk
   to?"

If they ask to see it, send the warm-invite URL during the call. By
the time you hang up they're inside their own demo pool.
