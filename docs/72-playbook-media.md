## 2. Why this works for media

Media partners have a problem most clubs do not have: **they need new content, every day, for six weeks**. WC2026 is the biggest possible content window in football, and every existing competitor (Goal.com, ESPN, Sky) will be running their own tournament tracker.

A Tournamental co-branded pool gives the media partner:

- A **proprietary leaderboard** that is theirs, not somebody else's tracker.
- A **content unit** for every match: "your bracket vs the consensus," "your top 10 listeners' picks vs ours."
- An **affiliate revenue stream** they did not have (Tournamental shares the kit-retailer affiliate revenue from share cards).
- A **brand re-engagement loop** for six weeks straight.

The pitch lands because every media partner is already trying to fill the WC2026 content window. We are not competing with their tournament tracker, we are giving them a complementary product they can promote on every episode.

## 4. The two paid options on top of the free tier

Most clubs/federations get pure free. Media is the segment where premium has natural buyer interest:

### Option A: Sponsored co-branded pool (free to media, paid by their sponsor)

One of the media partner's existing sponsors carries the pool as a "{{SPONSOR}} x {{COMPANY}} bracket." The sponsor pays the media partner their normal sponsorship rate; Tournamental gets a small per-active-user fee from the sponsor (negotiated separately). Free for the media partner.

This is the cleanest play for established podcasts / YouTubers who already have a sponsor inventory.

### Option B: White-label premium tier (paid by media)

Media partner pays a flat monthly fee for the duration of the tournament (~$400 to $1,200/month depending on audience size). In exchange:

- Zero Tournamental branding visible anywhere on the pool.
- Custom subdomain (`pool.theathletic.com` style).
- All affiliate revenue retained by the media partner.
- Premium dashboard for the media partner with audience demographics, engagement curves, share-graph data.

Best for: large media operations that want the pool to look like a native product.

### Option C: Standard tier (free, default)

- Tournamental small footer mark on share cards.
- Affiliate links on share cards split with the media partner.
- All other features identical.

Most replies will land here. Position the upgrade only after the first yes.

## 5. The content units we hand them

Every co-branded media pool comes with a content kit. Pre-baked, ready to publish:

- **Pre-match graphic**: "Audience consensus pick + odds." Drop into their YouTube thumbnail or Instagram story. Updated nightly via a CDN URL.
- **Mid-match leaderboard graphic**: live-updating, ready for stream overlay. WebSocket fed.
- **Post-match recap**: "Your audience picked {{TEAM}} to win. Here's how they did." Sent to the media partner as a templated draft email / post.
- **Daily content prompt**: "Today, ask your audience: who's most likely to score the opening goal? Use Tournamental's group-pick feature to capture the answer." Three of these per week.

This is the single biggest differentiator versus a plain pool. Media partners value pre-baked content units more than the leaderboard itself.

## 10. Objections + answers

**"We already have a tournament tracker."**

The pool is not a tracker, it's a community leaderboard. Co-exist. Most media partners want both: tracker for the news; pool for the community story.

**"We don't run user-generated content; legal won't like it."**

The pool doesn't show user-generated content publicly. It shows a leaderboard (names + bracket scores). No comments, no posts, no avatars from users. The media partner can opt out of every UGC surface if they prefer.

**"Our audience is too small to make this worth your time."**

Not true. The 60-word short-variant pitch (in `_email-variants.md`) is calibrated for sub-10k audiences. Even a 5k-subscriber Substack with 30 engaged members in a pool produces 30 daily-engagement loops. The host-level standard tier is free, so the only cost to them is sending the invite.

**"Can our sponsor's logo be exclusive on the pool?"**

On the standard tier we maintain category exclusivity (no other kit retailer affiliate while your sponsor is a kit retailer). On the premium tier, full custom branding control. Premium tier solves this for large media.

**"How fast can you build it?"**

24 hours for standard / premium. 30 minutes for self-serve.

**"Do we own the audience data?"**

Yes. Every audience member who joins via the media partner's pool is the media partner's contact, not Tournamental's. Tournamental holds it for the tournament window then deletes per the DPA.

**"What about commercials between our content kit and Tournamental's affiliate revenue?"**

On Option A: media partner's sponsor pays them direct, Tournamental gets a small per-active-user fee separately. On Option C: media partner gets 50% of affiliate revenue from share cards (Tournamental retains 50% for platform costs). On Option B: media partner gets 100%, Tournamental retains the monthly subscription.
