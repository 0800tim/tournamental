# 63, VSL script + production brief

> The 4-minute video sales letter is the highest-leverage asset in
> the 14-day blitz (doc 62, Playbook G). Every cold send, press
> pitch, and corporate DM carries the VSL link. Founder-to-camera,
> deliberately unpolished, recorded on a phone. This doc has the
> script, the shot list, the production checklist, and the
> distribution plan.

## Strategic frame (one paragraph, internalise before recording)

This isn't a product demo. It's a **competence proof for the
consulting offer**, delivered through the lens of a free gift to
the NZ market. The viewer should finish the 4 minutes thinking
"that's a guy who knows audience engagement; if I had a campaign
to run I'd talk to him." Tournamental itself is the demonstration
material. The platform's existence + the casual confidence Tim
shows on camera while explaining it is the entire pitch for Growth
Spurt Agency. **Never sell the consulting directly in the video** —
mention it once, in passing, in the last 30 seconds.

## Script: 4 minutes, 5 beats

### Beat 1, the hook (0:00 to 0:25)

**To camera, no intro, no music, eye contact:**

> If you're running a business with 20-plus staff, a real estate
> agency, a social club, or you're the marketing lead at a brand
> that wants to do something for the World Cup — I just built you
> a free tool. There's no catch, no upsell, no money flowing
> through it. Let me show you what it does in about a minute.

(*Tone: warm, casual, like you're talking to a mate. Not a
salesperson. Not formal.*)

**Note:** the first 7 seconds matter most. The phrase "I just
built you a free tool" is the hook — most people stop scrolling
when they hear "free" without "trial".

### Beat 2, the demo (0:25 to 1:30)

**Cut to screen recording, voiceover continuing:**

> This is the join page for a pool I set up for The Crate, which
> is an office in Albany. If their office manager has my colleague
> Andy's name, mobile and email, they paste them into this URL —
> [show typing] — and Andy gets a link.

[Show: warm-invite URL being constructed, then the join page
loading with Andy's name and the branded header.]

> He taps it. He gets a code on WhatsApp and email within 10
> seconds. Pastes the code. He's in the pool.

[Show: WhatsApp notification arriving on phone, code entered,
"You're in the pool" screen.]

> No app to download. No account to create. No password. Andy
> didn't even type his name. He's now seeing every match of the
> World Cup with a place to make his picks.

[Show: bracket builder, then the leaderboard with a few names on
it.]

> The office manager just emails the same link to 30 staff using
> their normal mail-merge tool — HubSpot, Mailchimp, HighLevel,
> whatever they use — and the office has a sweepstake running in
> five minutes flat.

(*Tone: matter-of-fact, almost surprised at how simple it is.
You're not selling — you're showing.*)

### Beat 3, why this matters (1:30 to 2:30)

**Back to camera:**

> The thing big brands always get wrong during the World Cup is
> they treat it as a one-shot. They post a meme on day one, they
> sponsor a screening on day three, they print a poster, and it's
> done. What actually works is touching the same audience five to
> seven times across the tournament with content they ASKED for.
>
> A prediction pool does that for you, automatically. Every time
> a match settles, every member gets a notification. Every week
> the leaderboard reshuffles. By the final, your audience has
> heard from you 20 times — and they wanted every one of those
> touches because they were invested in their own picks.
>
> Real estate agents, this is your Q2 audience touchpoint that
> doesn't feel like spam. Office managers, this is a five-minute
> setup that gets your whole team talking at the coffee machine
> for a month. Football clubs, this replaces your tipping
> spreadsheet for free.

(*Tone: confident, expert voice. This beat is where the consulting
competence shows through. You're not just demoing software, you're
naming the strategic insight that any decent marketing director
already half-knows but hadn't actioned.*)

### Beat 4, what it is and isn't (2:30 to 3:15)

**To camera:**

> Three things you should know about this thing.
>
> One. It's open source — Apache 2.0 licensed. The whole codebase
> is on GitHub. If I disappear tomorrow, you can still run it
> yourself.
>
> Two. There's no money flowing through it. If you want to put a
> prize on it — a long lunch, a $500 voucher, a weekend away —
> you organise that yourself. We never see the money. You stay
> in control of your members and your money.
>
> Three. It runs on whatever device the recipient has. iPhone,
> Android, laptop. No app store, no install, just a web link.

### Beat 5, the close + soft consulting plug (3:15 to 4:00)

**To camera, slow down here, eye contact:**

> So that's it. If you'd like a preview pool for your office or
> your business or your club, just reply to whoever sent you this
> video and they'll set one up for you in two minutes. Or go to
> play.tournamental.com and do it yourself.
>
> Quick thing about me: I run a CRM and audience-engagement
> consultancy called Growth Spurt Agency in Auckland. We help
> companies build the kind of always-on engagement systems that
> a prediction pool is one small example of. If after running
> this you find yourself thinking "we should be doing this all
> year, not just for the World Cup" — that's the conversation I
> have with my consulting clients. Reach out.
>
> Whoever wins your pool, you give them the prize. Whoever wins
> my tournament, gets to brag for four years. Good luck.

(*Tone: warm, direct. The consulting plug is 15 seconds, takes up
about 6% of the runtime, and is framed as "if you have THIS
follow-on question, here's where I come in" — not as a hard sell.*)

## Shot list

| Beat | Shot type | Length | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | To camera, head + shoulders | 25s | Plain background, natural daylight, no music |
| 2a | Screen recording (typing the warm-invite URL) | 15s | Use OBS, 720p, no cursor highlight effects |
| 2b | Screen recording (join page loading + code arrival) | 30s | Show real WhatsApp notification arriving |
| 2c | Screen recording (bracket + leaderboard) | 20s | Pick a pool with at least 5 members so the leaderboard reads |
| 3 | To camera, same setup as 1 | 60s | Slight zoom-in compared to Beat 1, signals shift to "now I'm being serious" |
| 4 | To camera, same setup | 45s | Could intercut quick B-roll of the GitHub repo on screen |
| 5 | To camera | 45s | Same setup, slightly closer framing |

**Total runtime: ~3:50, allow up to 4:15 with pauses.**

## Production checklist

**Gear (all owned, no purchases needed):**
- [ ] iPhone on a tripod (or a stack of books at eye level)
- [ ] AirPods Pro for audio (NOT iPhone built-in mic — even AirPods is better)
- [ ] Natural daylight from a window facing you
- [ ] OBS Studio for the screen recordings (free)
- [ ] CapCut for the edit (free)

**Pre-record (do tonight, 30 min):**
- [ ] Set up a real pool for "The Crate" with logo + 8-10 fake members named like real Aucklanders. The pool needs to LOOK lived-in for the demo to land.
- [ ] Compose a real warm-invite URL for "Andy Prentice" — he's already in your DB, use his real contact info.
- [ ] Practice the script once. Don't memorise — you should sound like you're explaining it to a mate at a barbecue, not reciting.

**Record (tomorrow morning, 90 min):**
- [ ] Shoot each beat 2-3 times. Pick the best take per beat in the edit.
- [ ] Screen recordings: do these AFTER the camera takes so you're warmed up.
- [ ] If you stumble in the middle of a take, just pause for 2 seconds and re-start the sentence — you can cut around it.

**Edit (tomorrow midday, 60 min):**
- [ ] CapCut: drop the camera takes in order, drop the screen recordings into the gaps.
- [ ] No music. No fancy transitions. Hard cuts only.
- [ ] Captions on (CapCut auto-generates them — sanity-check the spelling of "Tournamental" and "Growth Spurt").
- [ ] Export 1080p, 30fps, .mp4.

**Publish (tomorrow afternoon, 30 min):**
- [ ] Upload to YouTube as **unlisted**, NOT public. Title: "Tournamental: a free WC2026 office sweepstake tool (4-min demo)". Description: 1 paragraph + the warm-invite URL pattern.
- [ ] Also upload to LinkedIn natively (separate file, LinkedIn promotes native uploads over YouTube embeds).
- [ ] Make a 30-second cut of just Beat 1 + Beat 5 close. Use this on Twitter / X.
- [ ] Make a 60-second cut of Beats 2 + 5. Use this on Reddit + WhatsApp shares.

**Distribute (tomorrow afternoon onward):**
- [ ] Add the YouTube link to your Growth Spurt email signature.
- [ ] Add to play.tournamental.com homepage (`<a>` link in the hero, or embed if it doesn't slow LCP).
- [ ] Every cold send from now on includes the line "60-second walkthrough: [VSL link]".
- [ ] Pin the LinkedIn post for the next 14 days.

## What NOT to do in the VSL

- **Don't apologise for the production quality.** Lo-fi founder videos consistently out-perform high-production promos. Audiences are skeptical of polished — they trust scrappy.
- **Don't show the bracket builder for more than 20 seconds.** Most of your audience doesn't care about the picks-making UX. They care about "audience touched 7 times in 28 days."
- **Don't mention Anthropic / Claude / AI in the video at all.** It's not relevant to the buyer; mentioning it triggers the "AI hype" filter that some buyers reject on reflex. Save it for the press angle.
- **Don't quote pricing for the consulting.** The plug is "if you have this question, get in touch." A price tag in the video would feel like a different video entirely.
- **Don't add a CTA card at the end.** The voiceover IS the CTA.

## Iteration

If after the first 50 sends the reply rate is below 5%, the first
fix is **the first 7 seconds**. Re-record Beat 1 only with a
sharper hook. The rest of the video almost certainly doesn't need
to change — the hook is what wins or loses on view-through.

Suggested A/B alternatives for Beat 1:

- "If your office runs a World Cup sweepstake on a shared spreadsheet, you can throw the spreadsheet away."
- "I just spent 6 weeks building a free tool that replaces every World Cup office pool spreadsheet in the country."
- "Your office is going to run a World Cup sweepstake. Here's the version that's actually fun."
