# Playbook: Academies and Youth-Soccer Organisations

**Audience:** Football academies, coaching schools, youth-soccer associations, college / university soccer programmes, holiday-camp operators, football charities. **158 verified emails** in the dedicated academy list, plus the youth-club rows inside the country-specific clubs lists.

**The promise to an academy:** A free, family-friendly prediction pool every member's family joins. Kids pick the matches with their parents. Six weeks of academy-talking-point at every kitchen table. You set the prize. We do everything else. Not a betting product.

## 1. Who they are

Six sub-audiences:

1. **Independent football academies** (privately owned, fee-paying): PRO Player Academy, Coerver Coaching, Brazilian Soccer Schools, Football Talent Group, etc.
2. **Club-affiliated academies that take outside students**: Chelsea Academy non-residential, PSG Academy, FC Barcelona Camp Nou Experiences, Inter Miami CF Academy, Manchester United Soccer Schools.
3. **National youth-soccer governing bodies**: US Youth Soccer (3M+ players), AYSO, US Club Soccer, Canada Soccer Youth, The FA Youth + Skill Centres, FFF Youth FR, DFB Junioren DE.
4. **State / provincial / regional youth associations**: Cal-North Youth Soccer, Ontario Youth Soccer, Federazione Italiana Calcio Settore Giovanile e Scolastico, Cal South Youth, North Texas Youth, Florida Youth Soccer.
5. **College and university soccer programmes**: NCAA D1 + D2 + D3 men's + women's, BUCS UK, U Sports Canada, CBU Brasileiro Universitário.
6. **Football charities and CSR organisations**: Street Soccer USA, Show Racism the Red Card, Football for Hope, Common Goal, Soccer Without Borders, The Real Madrid Foundation Sociodeportiva.

## 2. The family-unit math

Where clubs reach members one-by-one, academies reach **families** of 2 to 4. Each academy kid = 1 to 3 parents + occasionally siblings + extended family. Net engagement reach per "academy member" is 2.5x to 4x.

| Academy / org | Direct members | Family-unit reach (2.5x) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Real Madrid Foundation Sociodeportiva | 85,000 kids | 200,000+ |
| US Youth Soccer | 3,000,000 players | 7,500,000+ |
| Coerver Coaching global | 200,000 kids across 35 countries | 500,000+ |
| Benfica Campus / Escolas | 50,000 kids, 250+ academies | 125,000+ |
| Cal South Youth | 250,000 registered players | 625,000+ |
| FIGC SGS Italy | 700,000 youth + 8,000 schools | 1.75M+ |
| Cruyff Foundation | 250,000 kids across 24 countries | 600,000+ |

A single academy yes = 100 to 200 family-unit emails of audience. A federation-level youth yes = millions.

## 3. Positioning

**One sentence**: "A free, family-friendly WC2026 prediction pool every family at {{COMPANY}} joins. Kids pick with parents, you set the prize. Safe, no money, not a betting product."

**Three reasons why now**:

1. **Six weeks of dinner-table talking points** about {{COMPANY}}. Lapsed parents come back into the conversation, active families feel more connected.
2. **It is not betting.** Picks-and-bragging-rights only. Age-gate compliant in every jurisdiction we operate. Safe for under-13s with parental consent.
3. **Recruitment hook for the new term.** "Join {{COMPANY}}'s WC2026 family pool" is a fantastic top-of-funnel for the September intake.

## 4. The not-a-betting-product position

The single biggest objection in academy outreach is "is this gambling?" The answer is unambiguous no, and the pitch must lead with it:

- **No money flows on Tournamental's side.** No entry fees, no winnings, no payouts.
- **The prize is academy-controlled** (kit voucher, holiday camp spot, free term, signed coach photo). Academy ships the prize.
- **Age-gate compliance**: under-13 accounts require parental consent at signup. Standard COPPA / GDPR-K / NZ Privacy Act safeguards.
- **No betting odds shown as bets.** We show predictions as picks. Visual + UX language is "pick" not "bet".
- **Not regulated as a gambling product** in any of the 13 priority countries. We have legal opinions on file for UK, USA, Canada, Brazil, Argentina, Portugal, Spain, Germany, France, Italy, Netherlands, Mexico, NZ.

Every academy pitch leads with this paragraph. No exceptions.

## 5. The prize recipe per academy tier

| Tier | Prize | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Independent academy | A free week of holiday camp + signed academy kit | Inventory the academy already provides; high perceived value to family. |
| Club-affiliated youth section | Match-day VIP experience (kid + parent) | Inventory the parent club already has; magical experience for the family. |

Again: academy picks and ships the prize. Tournamental never holds anything.

## 6. The 5-minute self-serve flow (works for academies too)

Same flow as clubs. Academies will mostly self-serve because they have organised marketing/comms teams and they already know their families well enough to write the prize copy themselves. Concierge requests will come from the larger national bodies and the charities (where compliance review is heavier).

## 7. The concierge build for academies

Academy concierge adds three things beyond clubs:

- **Family-mode toggle** on the pool (defaults to "parent fills bracket together with kid", optional "kid-mode" with simplified UI).
- **Family-safe share cards**: branded for academy, no affiliate kit-retailer links on share cards (we suppress those for under-13 audiences).
- **Comms kit for parents**: a one-page PDF the academy emails to parents explaining the pool, the safety considerations, and the prize.

Concierge time: 60 minutes per academy pool.

## 8. Launch timeline per academy

Day 0: cold email lands.
Day +1 / +2: reply, generally from a marketing or community-engagement lead.
Day +2: pitch call OR self-serve decision.
Day +3: pool live, parent-comms PDF delivered.

Daily: WhatsApp + email reminders to parents (with kid-friendly content tone).
Week 6: prize ceremony at academy. Photo opp for academy socials.

## 9. Content angles for the academy's channels

Pre-built content kit:

- **"Kid vs Parent leaderboard"** social post. Updated weekly. The most engagement-positive content the academy will post all year.
- **"Coach's bracket"** post: the head coach fills their bracket on camera, posts a one-minute video. Parents and kids respond with theirs.
- **Match-day reaction reel**: kids reacting to their pick winning or losing. Authentic, viral-ready.
- **Family of the week**: feature one academy family per week. Recruitment win.
- **End-of-tournament prize ceremony**: filmed, sent to parents, posted to academy socials. Closes the loop, hooks renewals.

## 10. Objections + answers

**"Is this safe for children?"**

Yes. No money, no betting, no real-name disclosure required, parental consent for under-13 accounts, COPPA / GDPR-K / NZ Privacy Act safeguards in place. We can sign a parental-consent process MOU with the academy if it helps. (Almost never needed.)

**"Will my parents need to download anything?"**

No. The pool is a web page, no app. Works in any browser on any phone.

**"What if my kid forgets to fill out their bracket?"**

We send a friendly WhatsApp + email reminder to the parent the day before kickoff for each match. Parent fills it on their phone in 30 seconds.

**"Can we use this for recruitment?"**

Yes. "Join our pool" is a top-of-funnel offer that converts. Free for new families to join (the pool, that is) - the academy decides whether to convert from pool joiner to academy member separately, on their own terms.

**"What if our families don't speak the same language as the platform?"**

play.tournamental.com renders in 20+ languages, auto-detected from the browser. WhatsApp + email reminders are in the recipient's preferred language. Academy can pick a default language for the pool (English, Spanish, Portuguese, French, Dutch, Italian, German, Japanese supported out of the box).

**"Will this distract our kids from training?"**

It's a 30-second-per-day touch, plus the social conversation. If anything it strengthens the academy's social currency among kids. (We have data from the NZ pilot showing pool-participating kids attend slightly more training sessions during the tournament. Will share on request.)

**"Can we charge parents an entry fee to participate?"**

We do not recommend it for academies (defeats the safe-not-gambling positioning). If the academy wants to do a charity donation drive alongside the pool (parents donate £5 to a chosen charity, academy matches it) we can wire that up as a side feature with the academy's payment processor (Tournamental never touches the money).

## 12. PR angles for academies

Academies have rich local-paper PR potential:

- **"Local academy launches free WC2026 family pool"**: suburban paper goldmine.
- **"50 families at {{ACADEMY}} pick the WC2026 winners"**: photo of a group of parents and kids holding a chalkboard with their picks.
- **End of tournament**: "The 8-year-old who picked {{TEAM}} to win"; photo with the academy coach and the prize.
- **Charity angle**: academy nominates a children's football charity for a 10% donation from the prize-pool sponsor. Both academy and charity get press.

## 13. Numbers we expect

- Cold-email reply rate to academies: **8 to 15%** (high because the family angle is fresh and few competitors are pitching it).
- That gives **12 to 25 academy yeses** from the 158-email list.
- Of those, ~30% will be national youth bodies (multiplier yeses, similar to federations).
- Each independent-academy yes = 50 to 500 family-unit pool members.
- Each national youth body yes = 5,000 to 500,000 family-unit pool members (USYSA scale).
