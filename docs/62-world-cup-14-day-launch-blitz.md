# 62, World Cup 14-day launch blitz playbook

> **Window: 2026-05-28 → 2026-06-11 (kickoff).** Everything in this doc
> is scoped to what realistically moves the needle in 14 days. Each
> playbook has a single lever, a real target list, a pitch script
> short enough to send today, and a concrete first action. The
> running thread across all of them is the warm-invite URL — you don't
> need recipients to sign up anywhere first.

## Strategic frame: this platform is the gift, the consulting is the ask

**Tournamental is 100% free, will stay 100% free, and you are not
trying to monetise it.** That's the truth. The reason this is a
launch-blitz priority anyway is because the platform doubles as a
qualifier for Tim's actual revenue engine:

> CRM + audience-engagement consulting for companies that run
> campaigns but don't have the systems to maximise them.

Every brand, real-estate brokerage, social club, and corporate that
runs a Tournamental pool is **demonstrating they care about audience
engagement** — they're self-selecting into the exact ICP for the
consulting offer. The free pool gives them value AND surfaces them
as a warm lead.

The pitch voice for every playbook below changes accordingly:

- **Don't apologise** for the platform ("I built this on weekends",
  "open-source side-project"). Own that you're a CRM consultant who
  open-sourced a tournament tool because the existing options were
  bad, and you're giving the platform to the market for the WC.
- **Don't sell the consulting in the cold send.** The free pool is
  the entire ask. The consulting comes out of inbound conversations
  AFTER they've seen you run the campaign well.
- **Do mention what you do** in your sig: "Tim Thomas, Growth Spurt
  Agency — CRM + audience engagement for NZ businesses." That's the
  hook, not the pitch.

The bet: out of 50-100 pool admins you set up by kickoff, 5-10 will
ask "how do you do this stuff for our actual marketing?" Those are
the engagements that pay for everything else.

## The asset that changed the maths

The thing that makes every playbook below work in this window is the
warm-invite URL shipped 2026-05-28:

```
https://play.tournamental.com/s/<pool-slug>/join?firstname=<f>&surname=<s>&mobile=<m>&email=<e>
```

Click it → branded pool header → "Welcome, <First Last>" → OTP via
WhatsApp + email arrives within 10 seconds → enter code → in the pool.
**Zero typing.** Anyone with a CSV of names/phones/emails can run a
mail-merge through their own CRM (HighLevel, Mailchimp, HubSpot,
Klaviyo, ActiveCampaign, raw SMTP) and the recipient is signed in in
under 30 seconds.

That single asset means the bottleneck is now **distribution, not
onboarding friction**. The playbooks below all attack distribution.

## What NOT to do in the next 14 days

Cut these from the to-do list, they don't pay back inside the window:

- **SEO content / blog posts.** Indexing takes weeks; ranking takes
  months.
- **Build a TikTok / IG following from zero.** Algorithm warm-up
  alone eats the window.
- **Cold paid ads (Meta, Google) without an installed pixel base.**
  First-day CPAs are 3-5x where they settle. Wait until after
  kickoff when you have a retention curve to optimise against.
- **App-store anything.** Review queues + onboarding friction kill
  the warm-invite advantage.
- **Influencer agency outreach.** They take a week to even reply.

What WORKS in 14 days: warm hand-offs from people who already trust
you to people who already trust them. Six playbooks for that.
