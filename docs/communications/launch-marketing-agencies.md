**Body**

Hi {{first_name}},

Six weeks of guaranteed daily attention from football fans is about to
go live: the 2026 FIFA World Cup&trade; kicks off June 11. Mexico vs
South Africa, Estadio Azteca, peak northern-summer prime time across
NA, EU, and APAC. Your clients are about to be drowned out by every
brand running variations of the same "guess the score" Instagram post.

There's a tidier way to ride the wave.

I've built [**Tournamental**][site], an open-source prediction game
for the 2026 World Cup. The bit that's relevant to your agency: any
client can run a **fully white-labelled prediction pool** as their
own customer-engagement campaign. You install the link (or embed
widget) on their site once; the platform handles every match for the
full six weeks.

**What you can sell into a client deck this week:**

- **A six-week always-on engagement loop.** Their customers pick every
  match, group stage through final, and can change any pick up to
  kickoff. That's 64 micro-engagement moments per player, not a
  one-shot bracket.
- **A branded landing page with their logo, banner image, primary
  colour, and a prize they choose.** Examples your clients will
  understand: a Shopify brand giving away store credit; a brewery
  giving away a keg to the streak leader; a fintech putting weekly
  &dollar;500 vouchers behind the top of their leaderboard; a B2B SaaS
  running it for their largest customer accounts.
- **A live leaderboard that gives the brand a reason to email + push
  every couple of days.** Built-in re-engagement copy. No content
  treadmill from the brand side.
- **Cryptographically verified picks.** Each player's bracket gets a
  hashed receipt before kickoff, so the brand can credibly say "we
  let our customers prove they called it". That's a headline your
  PR person can place.
- **Open source under Apache 2.0.** No platform-lock-in conversation,
  no per-seat fees, no "but what if Tournamental shuts down" objection
  from a paranoid head-of-legal. They can self-host the engine if they
  ever want to.

**Why this works for the agency, not just the client:**

- It's a finished product. You're billing for **setup + creative +
  prize sourcing + reporting**, not for the engineering. Your
  margins go up, your delivery risk goes down.

  shares &mdash; is yours to feed into your client's CRM. Real
  first-party data from a six-week campaign, not "they liked our
  post".
- It's seasonal. A clean wrap by mid-July leaves the team free for
  the rest of the calendar.
