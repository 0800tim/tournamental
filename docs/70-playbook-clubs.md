# Playbook: Football Clubs

**Audience:** Senior, amateur, grassroots, semi-pro, and pro football clubs across all 13 priority countries. From Auckland City FC to FC Bayern Munich. The single biggest segment in our 2,025-email blast list: **1,352 verified emails**.

**The promise to the club:** A free, fully-branded prediction pool that turns every match day of WC2026 into a club-touchpoint with your members, supporters, players, parents, and lapsed fans. You set the prize. We do everything else.

## 1. Who they are

Roughly five sub-audiences inside "clubs":

1. **Pro / semi-pro** with a sales team and a marketing budget. Inter Miami, Toronto FC, Auckland City, Benfica, Boca Juniors, Manchester United, the Bundesliga sides, the NWSL teams. They are pitched constantly. The bar is: must be free, must be visibly branded, must drive measurable engagement.
2. **Mid-tier semi-pro / academy-feeder clubs** (NPSL, NRFL, German Regionalliga, Brazilian Série C/D, Argentinian Federal A, NPL Australia equivalents). Small front-office team. A free branded pool is a real upgrade to their member-comms toolkit.
3. **Amateur senior clubs** with a volunteer secretary, a couple hundred members, and a Facebook page. The biggest single tier in the lists (~800 of the 1,352 entries). Easy yes, easy concierge build, brilliant local-newspaper PR angles.
4. **Junior / youth / academy sections of larger clubs**. Often run by parents. Family-unit reach: each kid is 2 to 4 emails of audience.
5. **Supporters' clubs / fan groups / overseas-fan branches**. Liverpool LA Supporters' Club, Boca Juniors USA, Croatian Auckland Supporters of Hajduk. Tight communities, ferocious engagement.

## 2. Why this works for a club specifically

A football club already has:

- A member or supporter list with an email column.
- A reason to email those members every week.
- A culture of "league table = identity".
- A prize they can dig out of the cupboard (signed kit, season tickets, match-day VIP).

What a club does NOT have:

- A six-week engagement loop they did not build themselves.
- A platform that turns the **other** World Cup (the one they are not in) into a story for their members.
- A way to reach lapsed members at no incremental cost.

The pool slots into the gap. Members come back every match day for six weeks; the club gets the audience attention without spinning up a tournament tracker, scoring rules, leaderboards, or a payment system.

## 3. Positioning

**One sentence**: "A free branded prediction pool that re-engages every member of {{COMPANY}} with the club for the entire WC2026, no money handling, you set the prize."

**Three reasons why now**:

1. **The window is closing.** WC2026 kicks off 11 June. After kickoff the urgency is gone.
2. **Members already want to talk about it.** The pool channels the conversation through the club's brand, not a generic bracket site.
3. **It is free.** Standard tier. No catches, no contract, no Tournamental cut of a prize that does not exist.

## 4. The prize recipe per club tier

The prize is what makes a club pool feel valuable to a member. Recommend based on tier:

| Tier | Prize | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pro / semi-pro | Signed home shirt + 2 tickets to a marquee match | Inventory the club already has, high perceived value, low cost. |
| Mid-tier semi-pro | Season pass for next year | High retention upside, costs nothing if the winner already buys. |
| Amateur senior | Club polo + 1 year free membership | Mostly costs the club a t-shirt. |
| Youth / academy | Half-term holiday camp spot or club kit voucher | Family value; kids and parents both happy. |
| Supporters' club | Trip to a home game (away supporters) or away-day travel kit | Identity-affirming, social. |

**Important:** the club picks the prize. Tournamental never holds it, ships it, or pays for it. This is non-negotiable for legal reasons and for the simplicity-of-pitch reason.

## 5. The 5-minute self-serve flow

We pitch this in every cold email. About a third of replying clubs will use it without us; the rest will ask us to build. Stripped to bare steps:

1. Open `play.tournamental.com/syndicates/new`.
2. Type the pool name (`{{COMPANY}}` or `{{COMPANY}}-wc2026`).
3. Upload the club logo (PNG, transparent background, 512x512 or bigger).
4. Pick the club's primary + accent colour from a colour picker (or paste a hex code).
5. Type the prize details into a free-text box.
6. Upload a banner image (1200x400, optional but worth it).
7. Save.

Done. Pool page is at `play.tournamental.com/s/<slug>`. Share URL ready to send to their member list.

## 6. The concierge alternative (for clubs that won't self-serve)

Most clubs will not self-serve. The reply we want is:

> "Sounds good, please set it up for us. Logo attached, colours are #ff0000 + #ffffff, prize is a signed Cole Palmer shirt + 2 tickets to the Spurs match in February."

We build it in 30 minutes. Reply with the pool URL. Done. Free.

Concierge metrics: target less than 24 hours from yes-reply to pool-live. Two of us can clear 30 to 50 concierge builds per day.

## 8. Content angles for the club's own channels

Lift these straight into the club's Instagram / TikTok / matchday programme / Facebook page once their pool is live:

- **"Our captain's bracket"** photo + bracket reveal post. Big engagement, costs nothing.
- **"Bracket of the week" leaderboard** in the matchday programme. Print or PDF.
- **"Pick versus consensus"** reel: "Three of our members picked Saudi Arabia to beat Spain, here's their reasoning". Match-day specific.
- **Rivalry split**: "Our youth team backs Argentina, our senior team backs Brazil. Updated leaderboard after every match."
- **Lapsed-member reactivation**: every member who never opened the original invite gets a friendly "you're missing out, here's the current top 5" nudge at week 3.

## 9. Specific objections + the answers

**"We already use FantasyFootball, why would we add another tournament product?"**

Fantasy is week-by-week and player-focused. A prediction pool is match-by-match and country-focused. They co-exist; one does not eat the other. Most clubs run both.

**"We're worried about the gambling angle."**

There is none. No money on Tournamental's side, no entry fees, no winnings except the club's own prize. We follow age-gate rules in every jurisdiction. The platform is explicitly NOT a betting product; we do not show odds as bets, we show predictions as picks.

**"Can our sponsor brand the pool instead of (or alongside) us?"**

Yes. Sponsor logo on every share card, sponsor copy in the prize block, sponsor link on the pool page. This is a standard concierge ask, we build it in the same one-day turnaround. Premium tier adds custom sponsor blocks; standard tier still allows one sponsor logo.

**"What if our member list is in a different country than the club?"**

Pool works for any geographic mix. Members get WhatsApp OTP (works globally) or email OTP (works globally). Daily reminders are sent in the recipient's local timezone. Leaderboard is global.

**"Can we white-label the pool entirely?"**

On the premium tier, yes. Standard tier shows a small "powered by Tournamental" footer on share cards (not on the pool page itself). For most clubs that footer is acceptable; premium is a paid upgrade.

**"How do you make money if it's free?"**

Standard-tier share cards carry opt-in affiliate links (kit retailers, ticket marketplaces). When a sharer's friend clicks through and buys, Tournamental and the partner split the commission. The club is never charged. Premium tier removes affiliate links for a monthly fee.

## 10. Success metrics the club will care about

Send these to the club after week 2 of the tournament as a "you might want to celebrate this" auto-email:

- Total members joined the pool.
- Average matches predicted per member (engagement depth).
- Number of new opt-ins to the club's mailing list via the pool (top-of-funnel).
- Share / re-share rate (viral coefficient).
- Country breakdown of members (for clubs with diaspora).

A standard report drops into the club's inbox at end-of-week 3 and end-of-tournament. Drafted template, club approves, fires.

## 11. PR play

Clubs in the smaller tiers love a local-paper story. Three angles that work:

1. **Local-business spotlight**: "Auckland City FC partners with Kiwi tech firm Tournamental to run a free WC2026 prediction pool for members." Pitch to the suburban newspaper / local sports column.
2. **Member-of-the-year angle**: at end of tournament, the winning member gets a photo with the captain. Local paper photo desk eats this for breakfast.
3. **Charity bolt-on**: club nominates a 10% donation to a chosen charity from the prize-pool sponsor cash. Charity angle softens the cold-email response from member secretaries who would otherwise ignore us.

## 12. Numbers we expect

Based on the agent-flagged top-10 lists per country and the historical Tournamental NZ-pilot data:

- Cold-email reply rate to clubs: **5 to 10%** for pro / semi-pro, **1 to 3%** for amateur. Volume weighted average across the 1,352 emails: ~2.5%.
- That gives **roughly 35 to 50 club replies** in the first 72 hours.
- Of replies, ~70% will be "yes-concierge", ~20% will be "yes-self-serve", ~10% will be "interested, tell me more".
- Final concierge build queue: **25 to 35 club pools** to build inside the 48 hours after wave 2.
- Pool conversions to active members (member joins / pool sends): **15 to 35%** historically.
- Active members per pool average: 80 (amateur) to 600 (pro).

Total active-member estimate inside week 1: 2,000 to 18,000 across all club pools.
