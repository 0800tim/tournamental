**Auckland, New Zealand, 13 May 2026.** Tournamental, an open-source tournament-prediction platform built for the FIFA World Cup 2026 and other major sports tournaments, launches today at [tournamental.com](https://tournamental.com) with the play surface live at [play.tournamental.com](https://play.tournamental.com), four weeks ahead of World Cup kickoff on 11 June 2026.

The launch is unusual in three ways.

First, every line of code, every design document, every marketing page is open-source under Apache 2.0 from day one. The repository at [github.com/0800tim/tournamental](https://github.com/0800tim/tournamental) contains the Next.js bracket app, the Astro marketing site, the Python StatsBomb replay producer that drives the 3D match renderer, the shared spec types, the on-chain VStamp signer, and roughly 80 design docs and session notes. Documentation is licensed under Creative Commons BY 4.0.

Second, contributors share platform revenue through [Drips Network](https://drips.network). There is no venture capital, no equity table, no employee-stock-option scheme to dilute. The upside flows on-chain to whoever shipped the work, by a public split formula that anyone can read, fork, or propose changes to.

Third, the entire system was built solo in 72 hours of focused work, with Claude as the engineering partner. The public commit history shows 28 pull requests merged in three days, ~80 docs and session notes shipped alongside the code, and a parallel-agent orchestration pattern any journalist can audit by reading the repository.

## What Tournamental does

Players make a bracket prediction for an entire tournament, the FIFA World Cup 2026 to start, and earn points as their picks come true. The game is built around six ideas that distinguish it from the established consumer-bracket products at ESPN, Yahoo, the Telegraph, and others.

- **Save, don't lock.** Players can change every pick up until the match they affect actually kicks off. Conviction-multipliers reward early saves, long-shot multipliers reward calling upsets the betting market did not see. The append-only ledger lets a player prove when they called something.
- **3D match renderer.** Every match plays out in a browser-based 3D renderer built on React Three Fiber, driven by StatsBomb open data for completed matches and live data feeds for upcoming fixtures. The full 2022 World Cup Final, Argentina 3-3 France (4-2 on penalties), plays at 10x speed in about 15 minutes.
- **Molecule pyramid.** A player's entire bracket renders as a 3D pyramid, one node per team per surviving stage, with a gold staircase rising from a 48-team base to a single champion atom at the apex.
- **VStamp on-chain receipts.** Every save is timestamped before kickoff and signed on-chain, so a tournament organiser, a sportsbook, or a friend can verify cryptographically when a pick was made.
- **Path-based syndicates.** Any organisation, brand, or individual can spin up `play.tournamental.com/s/<slug>` in seconds, with custom colours, custom share cards, and a private leaderboard.
- **Conversion-grade share cards.** Every save auto-generates a podium share card optimised for X, Telegram, WhatsApp, TikTok, and LINE, with the player's predicted champion and a verifiable `/s/<guid>` deep link.

## Why this launch matters

The bracket-prediction category is enormous and slow to innovate. The Telegraph Predictor ran 13.8 million entries on the 2022 World Cup. ESPN's bracket challenge does eight-figure monthly active users on March Madness. Yahoo's Premier League predictor, UEFA's Euro fantasy, Australia's NRL tipping, India's IPL fantasy that grew into the $6 billion company Dream11, every cycle of every tournament has the same pattern: closed-source apps, lock-once mechanics, no verifiable provenance, no contributor economy.

Tournamental's bet is that an open-source platform with save-don't-lock mechanics and on-chain provenance can compound across cycles in ways a closed app cannot. A developer in Sao Paulo can ship Portuguese commentary on day three of the tournament. A designer in Lagos can ship an African Cup of Nations share-card variant before that tournament starts. The marginal cost of each contribution is a code review, the marginal benefit can be a country's worth of new players.

## The 72-hour build

Tournamental is the work of one founder, Tim Thomas, working from New Zealand with Claude as the engineering partner. The build pattern is documented openly in the repository: an orchestrator agent owns the spec at `packages/spec/`, parallel builder agents each own one app directory and open pull requests, an admin merges on green local tests, and every change ships with a session note explaining the decisions behind it.

The 72-hour figure is auditable. Anyone can clone the repository and walk the commit graph from the initial commit through the 28 merged pull requests up to the launch tag. Every session note is in `sessions/`, every architecture decision is in `docs/`, and the failure modes are documented openly (production bugs, pm2 running from a deleted worktree path, agents stomping on each other's branches, a hydration crash that hit mobile mid-rebuild).

## Pricing and revenue

Tournamental's free tier covers the entire game loop: bracket creation, save-anytime mechanics, VStamp receipts, 3D match renderer, share cards, syndicates, leaderboards. Revenue comes from optional paid integrations (clip-pipeline boosts, syndicate-host upgrades, branded share-card themes) and affiliate-attributed sportsbook clicks. A fixed fraction of platform revenue flows on-chain to the Drips list that pays contributors. The Drips contract is public and auditable.

## About Tournamental

Tournamental is an open-source platform for turning any sporting tournament into a gamified prediction experience. Players save brackets, syndicates run their own pools, and every prediction is blockchain-anchored before kickoff for an immutable, independently verifiable record. The flagship launch is the 2026 FIFA World Cup, with the platform extensible to any single-elimination, group-stage, or league format a community wants to run: cricket world cups, rugby tournaments, tennis grand slams, esports majors, fantasy office leagues. Code is Apache 2.0, documentation is CC-BY 4.0, contributor revenue flows on-chain through Drips Network. Brand and affiliate codes are owned by Tournamental Holdings; everything else is public at [github.com/0800tim/tournamental](https://github.com/0800tim/tournamental). Headquartered in Aotearoa New Zealand. Live at [tournamental.com](https://tournamental.com).
