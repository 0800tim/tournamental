A prediction game lives or dies on its share card.

Bracket pools work because of the conversation around them вҖ” _"I had Argentina"_, _"You picked Mexico over France?"_, _"How does your bracket look at the half?"_ Those conversations happen on WhatsApp, Telegram, X, Instagram, TikTok вҖ” anywhere people argue about football. The platform that wants to be part of those conversations has to give players a single artefact they can drop into any of those threads and have it _make sense without the app open_.

For most prediction apps, that artefact is a screenshot of their bracket. Which is bad, because a wall-chart bracket looks like a spreadsheet вҖ” busy, unreadable on a phone, no visual hook.

This post is the design diary of what we shipped instead: a **podium share card** that puts your top three picks on a gold-silver-bronze stand with the country flags below each cup, your knockout path on the left, and a clean Tournamental wordmark in the corner. Three sizes for every social platform. Generated server-side in under 300 ms. Already triggering the share dialog from any pick row on the bracket page.

## The brief

When we redesigned the share card on 2026-05-11 the request from Tim was a one-paragraph piece of writing on a Slack thread:

> "It's not looking too great. Firstly, let's put the winner predicted on the right-hand side, and on the left-hand side have an image of the bracket picks leading up to that, maybe just the quarter-finals, semi-finals, and final. Choose who they selected as first, second, and third for the World Cup, so have a cup image: winner, silver, bronze. I want to show their free picks, and also the branding is not right."

Four asks in three sentences:

1. Winner on the right, not the centre.
2. Bracket path (QF вҶ’ SF вҶ’ Final) on the left.
3. Gold/silver/bronze cup imagery for the user's top three predictions.
4. Brand wordmark fixed (the previous build was rendering "TOURNAMENTВ·AL" with a stylistic middle dot from an older brand draft вҖ” should just be "Tournamental").

The fixes were quick. The interesting question was: **why this shape**?

## Why a podium is the right metaphor

A bracket prediction game has more than one prediction at its core. Players pick a **champion** (1st), but a thoughtful player also picks a **runner-up** (the team they think loses the final) and a **third-place winner** (the team they think wins the 3rd-place playoff). That is three distinct top-three picks, all visible to the cascade engine, all scored against actual outcomes.

A bracket _tree_ tells you the champion but hides the runner-up (it is "the team in the final's loser slot") and hides the third place (it is buried in a separate 3rd-place playoff fixture). A casual viewer cannot tell at a glance who you predicted to finish 1st / 2nd / 3rd.

A podium graphic _is_ that ranking, by definition. We have used the gold/silver/bronze trope for 2,000 years. Every viewer instantly reads "this person picked Argentina to win, France to come second, Brazil third." The card answers the question before the viewer has to think.

The left column ("Path to gold") is a four-row strip showing the user's pick at each knockout stage вҖ” R16, QF, SF, Final. For most cards these will all be the same team (the champion), but for a contrarian bracket it might be _"R16 ARG, QF ARG, SF MEX (the upset), Final MEX"_, which immediately tells the viewer this user called the Mexico-knocks-out-Argentina semi-final. The path is the **story**; the podium is the **answer**.

The right column ("My podium") is the centrepiece. Three cups, drawn procedurally in canvas (no image assets), arranged in a classic podium step: gold biggest and centred, silver to the left and slightly lower, bronze to the right and slightly lower. Below each cup, a flag chip with the country flag and a 3-letter code (ARG / FRA / BRA). Below the whole podium, the champion country name in big 56-px type with a "MY CHAMPION" gold pill underneath.

Three sizes ship out of the same renderer:

- **Portrait** 1080Г—1350 вҖ” Instagram feed, Facebook, generic share-sheet preview.
- **Landscape** 1200Г—630 вҖ” Twitter / X / Facebook OpenGraph, LinkedIn / Telegram link unfurl.
- **Square** 1080Г—1080 вҖ” Instagram square post, Slack / WhatsApp link unfurl.

Geometry is per-size; the visual hierarchy is identical. The square card uses a slightly tighter podium so the champion country name still fits below the cups. The landscape card stretches the left "Path to gold" column wider so the four stage rows breathe.

## The pipeline

This is a 2D canvas job, not a flexbox-and-HTML satori card. Trophies are rounded paths with handles and rims; flag rasters are 60Г—40 textured chips. None of that wants to be expressed as flexbox.

The render pipeline:

1. **HTTP request** lands at `apps/web/app/api/share/bracket/[bracketId]/[size].png`.
2. The route reads the user's saved bracket from KV (the same store the bracket UI reads).
3. It builds a `BracketShareCardInput` shape вҖ” champion, runnerUp, thirdPlace, knockoutPath array, tournament name, user handle, optional pundit chip.
4. Calls `renderBracketShareCard(input)` from `@vtorn/social-cards/canvas/bracket-share-card.ts`.
5. The function creates a `@napi-rs/canvas` instance at the size preset, paints the background gradient (the champion's kit colour seeds a radial-tint on the right side where the podium sits), then walks the layout: header вҶ’ bracket path strip вҶ’ podium вҶ’ footer.
6. Returns a PNG buffer. The route sets `Cache-Control: public, max-age=300, s-maxage=86400` so the same bracket+size is CDN-cached at the edge for a day.

Total time on a warm path: 80вҖ“120 ms. Cold path (first time generating a flag SVG into a PNG cache): 250вҖ“300 ms. Both well inside the budget for a share button that should feel instant on tap.

## The animated MP4 variant

The same scene composes into an animated MP4 share for stories (1080Г—1920) and TikTok (1080Г—1920). Same input, same layout, six-second reveal:

```
0.00s  Background gradient fades in.
0.25s  Header strip eases in. Wordmark + tournament title appear.
0.40s  "Path to gold" rows cascade in, one per stage, 50 ms apart.
0.55s  Bronze cup pops onto its step.
0.60s  Silver cup pops onto its step.
0.70s  Gold cup zooms in from above, lands on its step.
0.85s  Champion country name + "MY CHAMPION" pill swoosh in.
1.00s  Footer URL fades in.
1.00-5.50s  Hold on the full reveal.
5.50-6.00s  Soft gold-glow pulse, ready to loop.
```

Each beat is a `progress` value passed to the same `paintBracketFrame()` function that paints the static PNG. The video pipeline does not have to know the geometry of the card вҖ” the canvas renderer is the single source of truth. ffmpeg image2pipe encodes 360 frames at 60 fps into an H.264 MP4 with `+faststart` so it plays in-feed on Instagram and TikTok without buffering.

Total render time for the MP4: 3вҖ“4 seconds end-to-end. Cached as aggressively as the PNG.

## Why this is gamification, not marketing

This part matters. We did not build the podium share card to do "marketing". We built it because **the share card is part of the game**.

A bracket prediction game has three engagement loops:

1. **The pick loop** вҖ” open the app, browse a fixture, save a pick. We covered this in the [save-don't-lock post](/blog/2026-05-12-save-dont-lock).
2. **The watch loop** вҖ” load the 3D match renderer on a kickoff and watch your prediction land or fail. Live commentary, scrubber, replays.
3. **The brag loop** вҖ” share your bracket somewhere your friends will see it, then defend or revise it in the resulting argument.

Loop 3 is the one that brings other players in. Every share is a hook. The job of the share card is to be **legible** (you can read who picked what without zooming), **distinctive** (no one's podium looks identical to anyone else's), and **emotive** (the gold cup feels like a trophy, not a UI element).

The podium delivers on all three:

- Legible: a 12-year-old recognises the gold-silver-bronze layout.
- Distinctive: the country flags below each cup are unique to the user's picks; the path strip on the left tells a unique story about the road to the champion.
- Emotive: a glowing gold trophy with your country's flag below it is a small portrait of the future you want.

You cannot share a brag that nobody can read. The wall-chart is unreadable. The podium is unmissable.

## Open questions, deferred to the next sprint

- **Friend overlay**. Two podium cards side-by-side in one share вҖ” yours vs your friend's, with the divergence highlighted. This is the "WhatsApp group chat" surface in card form. Builds on the same input shape; just needs a second `BracketShareCardInput` and a wider canvas.
- **Day-by-day variant**. As the tournament progresses, the card should mutate. _"Day 7: Argentina out. Tim's bracket now shows Mexico for gold."_ A small "Updated 2 hours ago" chip and a `vs my original pick` diff strip. We have the history ledger from the save model; we just have not wired it into the card yet.
- **Pundit reputation badge**. Verified pundits get a small gold chip in the footer next to their handle. Already in the input shape (`pundit.level`); the renderer paints it; we need the pundit-onboarding flow before we turn it on for the public.

The card you see today is the v1. It will not be the v3. But it is enough to drive the share loop for the World Cup launch, and that is what we wanted to ship before the 30-day pre-kickoff window opens.
