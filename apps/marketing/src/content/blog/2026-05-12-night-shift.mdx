Seventeen PRs in twenty-four hours. One rewrote the renderer's
interpolation pipeline so the AR-FR demo no longer jerks on mobile.
Another turned every fixture in the app into a tap-anywhere pick
popup with a deep-linkable URL, so a friend can share
`/team/NZL?pick=55` and you land on New Zealand's page with the popup
already open on match 55. A third stood up the security pipeline that
gates the public-repo reveal. This is what happened.

## The renderer is smoother now

Tim watched the 2022 World Cup Final demo on his phone and reported
the field was jerking back and forth. The
[three.js skinning-blending example](https://threejs.org/examples/#webgl_animation_skinning_blending)
ran buttery on the same hardware, so we knew it was us, not the
device.

We walked the renderer + producer + store together and isolated four
root causes. The big one was inside the producer pacing loop: the
synthetic stream batches roughly four messages every fifty
milliseconds, but each state frame is one match-second apart. When
four frames land in the same wall-clock tick they all stamp the store
with the same wall-clock instant, so the renderer's interpolation
fraction saturates at one and the player just snaps to the latest
position. Players move four metres per match-second. Snapping four
metres every fifty milliseconds is the "teleport every tick" judder.

The fix is a per-match-time state frame buffer that interpolates on
position (linear), yaw (shortest-arc slerp), and the ball
(Catmull-Rom across four frames). On top of that we damped the
camera, clamped the useFrame delta to one-thirtieth of a second on
stalls so a tab-switch no longer punches a visible jump into the FSM,
doubled the pitch canvas to 2048x1024 with explicit mipmaps to kill
the grass shimmer, throttled the crowd material updates to four times
a second, and capped mobile DPR per profile. Twenty-three new tests
cover the buffer and damped-camera driver. The full diagnosis is in
`docs/43-renderer-fidelity-overhaul.md`.

## You can pick from anywhere now

The bracket page is the right surface for the lock-it-all-in flow.
But team pages, match preview pages, and (later) social cards
present an individual fixture. Tim's ask: "as you're browsing and
looking at teams and matches, tap on each of those and pop up just
that match and predict that match, like changing your score."

Two pieces shipped together. A `MatchPickPopup` component with three
presentation modes (bottom sheet on mobile, modal on desktop, inline
card for embedding) plus three new game-service endpoints: `PUT
/v1/picks/:userId/:matchId` to lock or change a pick, `GET` to read
it back, `DELETE` to remove it.

The lever is the persistence shape. A per-match write is a
read-modify-write on the same row the bulk submit handler touches,
so a user can pick game-by-game and later open the bulk builder to
find every pick already populated. Or they can submit the bulk
bracket and then change one pick game-by-game without re-submitting
everything.

The deep-link scheme is small but useful. `/team/NZL?pick=55` opens
New Zealand's page with the popup pre-opened on match 55. The URL
stays clean when you close the popup; back does the right thing.
That popup sits inside a broader `OverlayRouter` that encodes the
open overlay stack on the URL. The underlying page route does not
change when an overlay opens, so search engines and analytics still
see `/world-cup-2026` as the canonical page. Browser back unwinds
the stack first, then leaves the page. Specs:
`docs/44-overlay-router-and-mobile-overlays.md` and
`docs/45-per-match-pick-popup-and-api.md`.

## Going public, safely

The repo goes public soon. Before that we need a defence-in-depth
pipeline that triages incoming PRs autonomously and escalates on red
flags. That pipeline is open as PR #111 and ships in observation
mode first. Two services: `apps/pr-triage-bot` scores incoming PRs
zero to one hundred from path classifiers, file types, and diff
size, posts a structured triage comment, and routes review requests
to CODEOWNERS for yellow and red verdicts. `apps/security-watchdog`
runs scheduled scans (daily on dependencies, weekly full-repo) with
severity-routed alerts via pluggable sinks: Slack, Discord,
Telegram, Aiva-SMS, email.

The threat model is the part worth saying out loud. PR titles,
descriptions, commit messages and diff content are treated strictly
as data. There is no path where a string from a PR can change agent
or workflow behaviour: comments are posted via `gh api` with the
body in a tempfile, all scanner output is schema-validated before
action, and the prompt-injection canary scans for known attack
patterns and only *flags*, it never auto-actions.

## Deploys are now Sdeal-style

We borrowed the deploy strategy from one of our other projects.
Same-host blue-green slot swap, smoke-test on the staging port
before swap, atomic swap of the symlink, then `pm2 reload`.
Worst-case perceived downtime is two to three seconds, often less.
Persistent `.next/cache` between builds turns a one-line typo fix
from minutes into seconds, and the build matrix is per-app and
parallel so when only the marketing site changed we are not
rebuilding the bracket. Architecture in `docs/47-cicd-pipeline.md`.

## Four new apps

`apps/wc2026-data` is the live-match-state scraper with adapters
for SportRadar, API-Football, and a mock for dev. Real keys are
env-flagged off; the mock is the default until we have a paid feed.
`apps/dm-poll-forwarder` adds Reddit, Mastodon and Signal to
yesterday's sixteen-channel DM-OTP login service by polling
unread-DM endpoints (none of those three push webhooks).
`apps/drips-bridge` is the contributor revenue-split scaffold for
the Drips Network integration; mock client today, real one when the
contract audit lands. `apps/native` is the Capacitor iOS and Android
shell that wraps the bracket app into installable binaries with
push, haptics and share.
