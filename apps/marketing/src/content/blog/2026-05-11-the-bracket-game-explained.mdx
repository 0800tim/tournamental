The bracket page at [`2026wc.vtourn.com`](https://2026wc.vtourn.com) has one job: turn the chaos of 104 World Cup matches into a single shareable scorecard that tells you, at any moment, how your calls compare against everyone else's. This post is the plain-language tour of how that works, because we tried explaining it on a phone call yesterday and realised even our friends did not all follow the bit about odds-at-lock.

## The format

The 2026 World Cup is the first to run on the new 48-team layout: **twelve groups of four**, then a 32-team round of 16, then quarters, semis, the third-place playoff, and the final. From a prediction-game point of view that is **104 picks** in total: 72 group-stage match outcomes, then knockouts.

For VTourn we ladder it like this:

- **Group stage**: pick the winner (or draw) of every fixture, plus rank the four teams in your group so the right two advance and the right two go home
- **Round of 32**: the bracket page renders the path; you call each tie
- **Round of 16, quarter-finals, semi-finals, third-place playoff, final**: same again

Every pick is its own row. Every row stamps the moment you locked it. Every match is locked at kickoff and never again — more on that below.

## The auto-pick cascade

Most people we talk to fall into one of two camps:

1. The completist who genuinely wants to call every game
2. The casual fan who has views on twelve matches max and zero patience for the other 92

The completist is fine. The casual fan was the design problem. So the bracket page has an **auto-pick cascade**: one click and we walk the entire knockout tree, pick the winners, populate the next round, walk it again, and lock the whole thing in. The default cascade uses **FIFA-rank tiebreakers** — higher rank wins, ties broken by goal difference, ties further broken by alphabetical order. You can override the tiebreakers from a settings drawer if you have a particular view.

Once the cascade has filled out your card, you can **go back and tweak any individual pick**. Change Argentina to lose to Mexico in round 1 and the cascade re-runs forward from there: the next round shows Mexico, the round after shows whoever Mexico beats, and so on. Nothing is set in stone until the moment of kickoff.

## The kickoff lockout

Every fixture has a `kickoff` timestamp. The instant the local clock reaches it, the row flips to a locked state, all the buttons disable, and the banner says: "Sorry — this match has already started. You can't change it now." The server enforces the same rule for the API: any submit that arrives after `kickoff` for a given fixture comes back with `match_already_started`.

This matters because the next mechanic only works if the stamp is honest.

## Odds at lock

When you lock a pick, we capture the **live W/D/L odds** at that exact moment from the same feed (Polymarket plus a sportsbook aggregator) the bracket page is showing you. That snapshot is stored next to your prediction.

A pick at long odds — Mexico over Argentina at 12% the night before — is worth a lot more if it lands than the same pick made on the morning of the match when half the world has already piled in.

This rewards two things we want VTourn to be about:

1. **Conviction**. If you believe in something three weeks out, before the herd, you should get the upside.
2. **Effort**. Predicting a tournament early is harder. We respect that with the points we give you back.

Hindsight bracketing is a fine pastime. We just do not score it the same way.

## The history ledger

Every change you make to your bracket — every individual pick, every group ranking, every auto-pick run — writes a small append-only entry to a local ledger. Open the **History** drawer on any match row and you see:

```
2026-05-08 14:22  ARG > MEX  → 1-0  (odds at lock: 47% / 31% / 22%)
2026-05-09 07:14  ARG > MEX  → 2-1  (odds at lock: 49% / 29% / 22%)
2026-06-13 19:55  LOCKED        — match has started
```

It is a small thing but it makes the game more honest. When the tournament ends and a friend tells you "I had Argentina nailed from the start," you can ask them to show their working. We will be the platform that does.

## The supporting pages

The bracket page is the main thing, but the same picks feed two other surfaces:

- **`/match/[id]/preview`** — every fixture has a five-tab preview built around it: Predict, H2H, Form, Lineup, and Stats. It is the page you open if you have not made up your mind yet. The Predict tab is the same row as on the bracket page; the others are the supporting evidence.
- **`/team/[code]`** — every team gets a detail page with a massive circular flag, the kit-colour gradient strip, the FIFA rank chip, recent form (W, D, L dots), the upcoming fixture deep-link, head-to-head, and a squad grid

Pick a route, follow the breadcrumbs, end up where you started.

## Closing the loop

When the tournament finishes the bracket settles. We score every pick against the actual result, multiply by the odds-at-lock multiplier, and rank everyone on the leaderboard. The winner of the global leaderboard gets the cleanest possible verifiable receipt: an on-chain VStamp with their full bracket signed at kickoff and their final score recorded. Bragging rights with a cryptographic seal.
